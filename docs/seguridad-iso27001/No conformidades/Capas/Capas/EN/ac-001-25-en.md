&lt;td>Description of the finding&lt;/td>
&lt;td colspan="12">The internal audit report lacks adequate process
coverage and objective evidence:
&lt;ul>
&lt;li>Only one person (Quality Director) was audited, limiting
verification of QMS implementation across functions. Furthermore, key
processes were not audited, including supplier control, complaint
handling, software validation, and internal audits.&lt;/li>
&lt;/ul>
&lt;ul>
&lt;li>The report does not provide adequate objective evidence (e.g.,
sampled records, checklists) to support conformity claims.&lt;/li>
&lt;li>The report and the agenda do not have identification as part of
the QMS system&lt;/li>
&lt;/ul>&lt;/td>
&lt;/tr>
&lt;tr class="odd">
&lt;td>Root cause analysis&lt;/td>
&lt;td colspan="12">The internal audit was carried out by only one person,
which limited the coverage and objectivity of the process. Furthermore,
due to partial planning, some relevant processes were not included. The
absence of objective evidence and the lack of formal identification in
the documentation are due to insufficient application of the current
audit procedure and the use of outdated templates.&lt;/td>
&lt;/tr>
&lt;tr class="even">
&lt;td>Immediate correction&lt;/td>
&lt;td colspan="12">Record the non-conformity in the quality system and
provide traceability of the follow-up.&lt;/td>
&lt;/tr>
&lt;tr class="odd">
&lt;td>Corrective/preventive action&lt;/td>
&lt;td colspan="12">Review and update the internal audit procedure
to:
• Include minimum criteria for process coverage and evidence
sampling, requiring all departments affected by the QMS to be
audited.
• Highlight the need to use controlled templates with identification
in accordance with the document management system.
• Ensure that future internal audits adequately plan the scope and
provide for the review of key processes.&lt;/td>
