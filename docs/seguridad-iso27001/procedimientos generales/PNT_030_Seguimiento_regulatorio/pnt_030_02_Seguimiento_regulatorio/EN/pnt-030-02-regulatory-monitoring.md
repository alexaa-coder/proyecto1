# Aim

Ensure that SPIKA systematically evaluates and implements changes in
standards, laws, and regulatory requirements applicable to its products,
ensuring continued compliance with:

- Regulation (EU) 2017/745 (MDR).

- ISO 13485:2016.

- Other applicable regulations (FDA, national, etc.).

# Scope

It applies to all of the organization's procedures related to the
implemented quality management system.

# Definitions and Acronyms

- **Regulatory change** : Modification in applicable laws, regulations,
 standards or guidelines .

- **Impact assessment** : Process to determine whether a regulatory
 change affects the organization's product, documentation or processes

- **AEMPS** : Spanish Agency for Medicines and Health Products .

- **BOE** : Official State Gazette

- **OJEU** : Official Journal of the European Union

- **MDR:** Medical Device Regulation

- NB **:** Notified *Body*

# Procedure

# Identification of regulatory changes

To identify regulatory changes, active monitoring is carried out through
periodic review every 6 months of official sources. This identification
and review of regulatory changes will be carried out by the quality
manager or the person delegated by him/her:

- Official Journal of the EU (quarterly)

- AEMPS website (quarterly)

- Publications of notified bodies (quarterly)

- ISO Standards Updates (Quarterly)

# Assessment of relevance and impact

When a potential change is identified, the following actions are taken:

1. The detected change is recorded in the online regulatory change
 detection form.

2. The team responsible for the quality management system and
 regulatory documents performs a preliminary analysis to determine:

 1. If it affects products (by type, class or market)

 2. The degree of impact (high, medium or low)

 3. The deadlines for implementing the change

# Planning and implementation

For changes that require action, a specific action plan is made that
includes:

- Documents to be updated (ETM, procedures, etc.).

- Necessary process modifications.

- Staff training needs.

- Realistic implementation deadlines.

# Verification and closure

Once all the changes have been implemented to comply with the regulatory
changes, the effectiveness of the change is verified by:

- Internal audits

- Review of document samples

- Surveys of trained personnel
