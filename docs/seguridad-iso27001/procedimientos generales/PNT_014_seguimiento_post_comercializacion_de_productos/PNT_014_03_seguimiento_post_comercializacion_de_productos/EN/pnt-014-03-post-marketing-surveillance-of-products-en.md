# Target

The purpose of this procedure is to describe how the post-marketing
management of medical devices supplied by Spika Tech, hereinafter
referred to as SPIKA, is carried out.

As set out in Regulation 2017/745 on medical devices, the post-market
surveillance system should be adequate to actively and systematically
collect, retain and analyse relevant data on the quality, performance
and safety of a device throughout its life cycle, to draw relevant
conclusions and to identify, implement and monitor any preventive and
corrective actions.

# Outreach

This procedure applies to all medical devices supplied by SPIKA.

# Definitions and Acronyms

- **Medical device (MD):** any instrument, device, equipment, software,
 implant, reagent, material or other article intended by the
 manufacturer to be used on humans, separately or in combination, for
 any of the following specific medical purposes:

&lt;!-- -->

- diagnosis, prevention, monitoring, prediction, prognosis, treatment or
 alleviation of a disease,

- diagnosis, follow-up, treatment, relief or compensation for an injury
 or disability,

- investigation, replacement or modification of the anatomy or of a
 physiological or pathological process or state,

- information obtained by in vitro examination of samples derived from
 the human body, including organ, blood and tissue donations, and which
 does not exert its intended primary action within or on the surface of
 the human body by pharmacological, immunological or metabolic
 mechanisms, but to whose function such mechanisms may contribute.

&lt;!-- -->

- **Medical device vigilance system:** The Medical Devices Vigilance
 System refers to the reporting of adverse incidents involving medical
 devices, their recording and evaluation, the adoption of appropriate
 measures for health protection and the transmission of these measures
 to the actors concerned.

- **Incident**: Any malfunction or deterioration of the characteristics
 or performance of a marketed product, including errors of use due to
 ergonomic features, as well as any inadequacy of the information
 provided by the manufacturer or any undesirable side effects.

- **Serious Incident:** To report an incident, it must be associated
 with a medical device or with the information provided with the
 medical device and the incident must be such that it has resulted in
 death or serious deterioration in health, or if it occurs again it may
 cause death or serious deterioration in health.

The types of adverse incidents to be reported are as follows:

1\. Those resulting in death

2\. Those resulting in a serious deterioration of the patient's, user's
or other person's state of health, such as, for example:

> a\) Life-threatening illness or injury
>
> b\) Permanent impairment of a bodily function or permanent damage to a
> bodily structure
>
> c\) A process that requires medical or surgical intervention to
> prevent permanent impairment of a bodily function or permanent damage
> to a bodily structure.

3\. Potential incidents, which are those that could have resulted in
death or serious deterioration of health, but did not occur due to
either fortunate circumstances or the intervention of healthcare
personnel.

# Procedure

# Introduction and generalities

SPIKA as a Manufacturer shall comply with the requirements on vigilance
of medical devices as laid down in the applicable legislation in force
for Class IIa products of which it is the manufacturer.

Information on adverse incidents related to medical devices manufactured
by SPIKA may come from:

- Healthcare professionals, directly or through any employee of SPIKA or
 service providers contracted by SPIKA

- Patients, users or private healthcare professionals, directly or
 through any employee of SPIKA or companies subcontracted by SPIKA.

- Companies with which SPIKA has marketing agreements

- Health regulatory authorities

If such information is received, a complaint report will be processed
according to **SOP-007 Complaint Handling** and the causes, consequences
and corrective actions to be implemented, if necessary, will be
investigated.

A post-marketing monitoring plan is drawn up at SPIKA that will collect
the following information:

- Information relating to serious incidents, managed through **SOP-007
 Complaints Handling**

- Records concerning non-serious incidents or data on undesirable
 effects of the product

- Information on trend reports

- Relevant bibliography, databases and/or specialist or technical
 records

- Information, including feedback and complaints from users,
 distributors and importers´

- Public information on similar medical devices

This monitoring plan includes proactive and systematic searches to
collect all the information mentioned above to characterise the
product's performance and compare it with similar products already on
the market.

# Incident management and reporting to health authorities

SPIKA shall manage reported incidents through **SOP-007 Complaint
Handling,** as soon as an incident is detected, an initial report shall
be sent to the competent national authority where the incident occurred.

Incidents categorised as serious will be reported to the authorities.
Products manufactured by SPIKA are Class IIa, however, they will be
monitored and any notification will be made using the MIR Manufacturer
Incident Report for Serious incidents and Incidents form, search for its
current version on the Internet ([MIR 7.3.1
Helptext](https://health.ec.europa.eu/document/download/dc6acc1b-f1ae-4512-b838-2fae869124c3_en?filename=md_new-reg_mir-help-v7.3.1_en.pdf&prefLang=cs)).

The Responsible Technician will be in charge of communications with the
Health Authorities.

# Communication deadlines 

SPIKA is obliged to report a confirmed serious incident immediately
after causality of the incident to the product has been established or
it has been established that such a causal relationship is reasonably
possible, and no later than 15 days after becoming aware of the
incident.

Incomplete preliminary reports can be submitted followed by the full
report.

# Monitoring and reporting plan

SPIKA undertakes to produce a report every six months with the following
content:

- Information relating to serious incidents, managed through **SOP-007
 Complaint Management,** shall list the complaints received indicating
 root cause and associated CAPAs, as well as whether they are
 associated with surveillance incidents or are quality defects.

- Records concerning non-serious incidents or data on undesirable
 effects of the product

- Information relating to trend reports,

- Relevant bibliography, databases and/or specialised or technical
 records will be searched:

&lt;!-- -->

- Information Notes that may be published on the websites of the Health
 Authorities for medical devices (EMA and AEMPS) and the WHO.

- Safety-relevant publications on PubMed
 [(](http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/)http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/)
 will be reviewed to be alert for possible incidents.

&lt;!-- -->

- Information, including comments and complaints from users,
 distributors and importers'.

- Public information on similar medical devices

 *With all the data collected in the post-market monitoring report
 SPIKA will be able to:*

&lt;!-- -->

- *improving benefit-risk assessment and risk management*

- *update information on design and manufacture, instructions for use
 and labelling*

- *updating the clinical assessment*

- *update the clinical safety and performance summary*

- *identify needs for preventive, corrective or safety corrective
 actions*

- *identify possibilities for improving the use, performance and safety
 of the product*

- *contribute, where appropriate, to post-market monitoring of other
 products*

- *spotting and reporting trends*

The information gathered in these reports will allow us to verify the
consistency of the product in terms of safety, efficacy and quality,
highlighting possible trends and allowing us to clearly identify the
improvements to be implemented, constantly evaluating the risk benefit,
the manufacturing process and therefore the quality of the final
product. The frequency of this report may be increased if in two
successive reports no potential risks or hazards related to the product
are found by increasing the frequency of the report by a further six
months, e.g. after two six-monthly reports with no potential risks, the
report shall be carried out every year and so on.

*In doing so, the updating of the technical documentation of the product
shall be carried out where applicable. Likewise, where necessary,
corrective actions shall be notified to the competent authorities
concerned.*
