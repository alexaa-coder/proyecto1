# Aim

The objective of this procedure is the system for selecting, evaluating
and approving Spika Tech suppliers, hereinafter SPIKA, establishing the
basic requirements for approving suppliers that lead to the affirmation
that:

- Approved suppliers are available who can guarantee the supply of
  products or the performance of activities that are to be
  subcontracted.

- Suppliers have the necessary requirements to provide a
  material/service with the required quality, in accordance with
  internal specifications and regulatory and customer requirements.

# Scope

The scope of this procedure applies to the selection, evaluation and
approval of suppliers, as follows:

| Supplier            | Material                                                                 |
|---------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Manufacturers       | Hardware components, external devices                                    |
| Software providers  | Cloud platforms, embedded software                                       |
| Service providers   | Validation, qualification, software testing, quality control, consulting |
| Logistics providers | Transportation, distribution or warehouse management services            |

# Definitions and Acronyms

- Software **:** A set of instructions, programs, or applications
  designed to perform specific tasks within a computer system.

- Software vendor **:** An external entity responsible for
  providing software or software components that may be integrated,
  used, or required in the development, validation, or maintenance of
  products or services.

- Cloud computing **:** A technology service delivery model that
  enables remote access, via networks, to shared and configurable
  computing resources (such as storage, servers, databases,
  applications, and services).

- Cloud provider **:** An organization that offers cloud-based
  services (such as AWS, Microsoft Azure, Google Cloud).

# Procedure

# Introduction

Having approved suppliers is essential to meet customer, regulatory, and
organizational requirements.

Furthermore, having approved suppliers prevents quality issues
associated with the products or services supplied, as well as ensuring
the search for alternative suppliers to ensure supply.

# Selection

Anyone within the organization can propose new suppliers by completing a
structured form. This form collects all the information necessary to
evaluate the supplier's suitability, including the materials or services
offered, associated risks, and selection criteria.

Suppliers with a direct supply chain and those with satisfactory prior
experience will be prioritized. The Quality Department will be
responsible for reviewing and validating the information received before
proceeding with the formal supplier evaluation.

# Assessment

Suppliers may be classified as critical or non-critical depending on
their impact on:

1.  **The quality management system** : suppliers whose products,
    materials or services are essential to meet regulatory and quality
    requirements.

2.  **Processes and activities** : suppliers that directly affect the
    manufacturing or servicing of the final product

3.  **The end product** : Suppliers that provide key components or
    services that impact the safety, efficacy, or regulatory compliance
    of the product.

Criteria for determining critical suppliers:

- **Product or material manufacturers:** Those who supply raw materials,
  key components, or packaging materials that have a direct impact on
  product compliance with regulatory requirements.

- **Service providers:** external consultants or services that affect
  validated or critical processes.

- **Distributors:** If they act as intermediaries for products that have
  a direct impact on final product requirements or regulatory
  compliance.

Evaluation system according to the type and impact of the supplier

**Critical Suppliers:**

A more thorough assessment is required due to the direct impact on
product and process quality. The documentation to be collected includes:

- **Quality Agreement** (See Annex 1): Defines the responsibilities and
  expectations between the parties.

- **Quality certifications:** ISO, GMP, GDP or other applicable
  standards.

- **Technical specifications or product sheets** (if applicable):
  Guarantees that the product meets the established requirements.

- **Declarations or additional documentation** : Declarations of
  conformity, audit reports, quality tests, or other documents specific
  to the product or service.

- **Audits (if applicable):** Conduct on-site or remote audits to verify
  compliance with quality standards and established agreements.

**Non-critical suppliers:**

For suppliers with a lower impact, a proportional assessment will be
applied, focusing on basic documentation:

- **Quality Agreement** (See Annex 1).

- **Quality certifications available.**

- **Minimum additional documentation** to validate that the products or
  services meet the established basic requirements.

**\*** *If the specified evaluation process is not possible, an
exception will be made by gathering all available information about the
vendor and conducting an assessment to determine suitability. This may
apply, for example, to software vendors or other specific cases.*

# Supplier approval

The Technical Manager or the person designated by them is responsible
for issuing a decision on the approval or rejection of an evaluated
supplier. They will issue their decision after reviewing the
documentation received from the supplier and preparing the form in
**Annex 2** , signing and dating the approval or rejection. The supplier
list will then be updated; a sample/template is included in **Annex 3**
.

A quality agreement template is proposed in **Annex 1 , although
agreements submitted by the supplier may be signed or modifications made
to the one included in this procedure.**

All generated documentation will be filed and safeguarded by the
Responsible Technician.

# 

# Re-evaluation of suppliers

Suppliers may be re-evaluated if:

- After inactivity of at least 5 years and they are required to apply
  again

- According to the history of complaints or incidents arising from them,
  to be concluded and reviewed in the Quality Management System Review
  Report by Management.

# Audits

In cases where the critical nature of the service provided and after
evaluating the available documentation, an audit will be conducted at
the supplier's facilities. The provisions of **PNT-013, Internal and
External Audits, will be followed.**

# 

# Documentation file

submitted documentation, audit reports, follow-up reports, annual audit
plan, etc.) will be kept in the files of the QA/RA department, as
