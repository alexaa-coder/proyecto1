# Aim

The objective of this procedure is the procedure for the selection,
evaluation, classification, approval and re-evaluation of suppliers that
supply products or services that affect the quality of the medical
device, ensuring their compliance with the requirements of the
organization and the applicable regulations.

# Scope

The scope of this procedure applies to all third-party providers that
supply products or services used in the development, validation,
maintenance, or support of the product developed by SPIKA.

# Responsibilities and Departments Affected

The Technical Manager or the person to whom he/she delegates will
approve or reject the evaluation of the suppliers and will safeguard all
the information and documentation generated.

# Definitions and Acronyms

- Software **:** A set of instructions, programs, or applications
 designed to perform specific tasks within a computer system.

- Software vendor **:** An external entity responsible for
 providing software or software components that may be integrated,
 used, or required in the development, validation, or maintenance of
 products or services.

- Cloud computing **:** A technology service delivery model that
 enables remote access, via networks, to shared and configurable
 computing resources (such as storage, servers, databases,
 applications, and services).

- Cloud provider **:** An organization that offers cloud-based
 services (such as AWS, Microsoft Azure, Google Cloud).

# Procedure

# Introduction

Having approved suppliers is essential to meet customer, regulatory, and
organizational requirements.

Furthermore, having approved suppliers prevents quality issues
associated with the products or services supplied, as well as ensuring
the search for alternative suppliers to ensure supply.

# Selection

Anyone within the organization can propose new suppliers by completing a
structured form. This form collects all the information necessary to
evaluate the supplier's suitability, including the materials or services
offered, associated risks, and selection criteria.

Suppliers with a direct supply chain and those with satisfactory prior
experience will be prioritized. The Quality Department will be
responsible for reviewing and validating the information received before
proceeding with the formal supplier evaluation.

# Supplier classification

| Type of provider | Material |
|------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Critical | Supplier whose products or services directly affect the design, development, validation, maintenance, support or fulfillment of the product |
| Uncritical | Supplier that does not directly affect the characteristics or performance of the product |
| Not evaluable | Large technology companies or government agencies whose processes are not available for direct evaluation, but whose services are essential. |

# 

# Initial evaluation

**Critical suppliers are required:**

- **Mandatory documentation** :

 - ISO 13485 (preferred) or ISO 9001 certification or other relevant
 certification (SOC 2, ISO 27001, etc.).

 - Signed quality agreement (Annex 1), NDA or documented acceptance of
 general conditions.

- **Technical evaluation** :

 - Assessment of the impact on the safety, integrity and performance of
 the medical device.

- **Audit** :

 - If feasible, a remote or in-person audit will be conducted.

**If the supplier does not have a quality certificate** :

- Its technical reliability will be assessed through tests, internal
 trials, references and experience.

- The justification for acceptance will be documented in Annex 2.

**If the supplier does not accept audit** :

- It will be documented as "Non-audited Supplier".

- **Reinforced controls** will be established , such as service
 validation, redundancy, and frequent integration tests .

**Non-critical suppliers are required:**

- Basic registration in Annex 2.

- Evaluation of previous history if you have already collaborated.

- No quality certificate or audit is required.

**Non-evaluable suppliers must:**

- Document in Annex 2 as “Not evaluable”.

- Attach general terms of service, security and privacy policies, and
 available public certifications (e.g., ISO 27001, SOC 2, CSA STAR,
 etc.).

- Compensatory internal control is applied:

 - Internal functional validations.

 - Continuous service monitoring.

 - Specific risk analysis.

# 

# Supplier approval

The Technical Manager or the person designated by him or her is
responsible for issuing a decision on the approval or rejection of an
evaluated supplier. This decision will be made after reviewing the
documentation received from the supplier, signing and dating the
approval or rejection. The supplier list will then be updated; a
sample/template is included in **Annex 3** .

A quality agreement template is proposed in **Annex 1 , although
agreements submitted by the supplier may be signed or modifications made
to the one included in this procedure.**

All generated documentation will be filed and safeguarded by the
Responsible Technician.

# 

# Re-evaluation of suppliers

| Provider Type | Initial Requirements | Re-evaluation | If you don't comply... |
|---------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------|----------------------------------------------------------------|
| Critical | ISO 13485 / ISO 9001 / Technical evidence / Contract / Audit if applicable | Annual | Accepted if there is technical validation / reinforced control |
| Uncritical | Annex 2 complete / Informal review | Every 3 years | It can continue to be used if there is no impact on quality. |
| Not Evaluable | Public Evidence / Security Certificates / Terms of Service | Annual | Accepted if compensating internal controls exist |

# Audits

Audits: Internal and External will be followed.**

&lt;ol type="1">
&lt;li>The supplier is open to on-site audits and/or

interfering with ongoing operations .&lt;/li>
&lt;li>The results of the audits and observations will be submitted by

implementation of any required corrective measures.&lt;/li>
&lt;li>The frequency of audits will depend on the results of the audits
and the quality of the supplier. If there are no critical
nonconformities regarding product quality, the frequency will not be
more than every three years.&lt;/li>
&lt;/ol>
**Article 4. Retention samples**
&lt;ol type="1">
&lt;li>The supplier will keep sufficient retention samples to repeat
analysis at least twice against specifications.&lt;/li>
&lt;/ol>
**Article 5: Documentation**
&lt;ol type="1">
&lt;li>The supplier will store the production documentation and all

&lt;li>The original documentation is accessible in the event that the
buyer attends the facilities for an audit.&lt;/li>
&lt;/ol>
**Article 6. Claims/Complaints**
&lt;ol type="1">
&lt;li>The supplier will respond to complaints submitted by the buyer
within a period of no more than 20 business days. In the case of serious
complaints, the maximum response time will be 5 days, in order to
deliver a written preliminary report and a final report within 10
days.&lt;/li>
&lt;li>In the event of non-compliance with the indicated deadlines, the
supplier may provide interim reports to the buyer.&lt;/li>
&lt;li>The supplier will inform the buyer if it receives serious
complaints with a high impact on the products supplied to the
buyer.&lt;/li>
&lt;/ol>
**Article 7. Withdrawal**
&lt;ol type="1">
&lt;li>In the event of a recall by the supplier, the buyer will be
immediately informed of the quality defects.&lt;/li>
&lt;li>In the event of a recall, the buyer and supplier will agree on
and decide on roles and responsibilities, as well as the coordination of
the investigation.&lt;/li>
&lt;li>The buyer is the one who will make the final decision on the
finished product based on scientific criteria and risk
management.&lt;/li>
&lt;/ol>
**Article 8: Raw materials**
&lt;ol type="1">
&lt;li>The supplier is responsible for purchasing, storing, analyzing,

current requirements.&lt;/li>
&lt;/ol>
**Article 9. Deviations**
&lt;ol type="1">

possible.&lt;/li>
&lt;/ol>
