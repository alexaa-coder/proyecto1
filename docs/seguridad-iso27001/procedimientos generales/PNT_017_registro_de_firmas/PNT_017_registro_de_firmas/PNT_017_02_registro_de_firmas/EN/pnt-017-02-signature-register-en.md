# Aim

Establish the use of electronic signatures linked to user accounts in
SPIKA's digital management system, ensuring the traceability,
authenticity, and integrity of the actions performed in the Quality
Management System (QMS), in accordance with ISO 13485 and Regulation
(EU) 2017/745.

# Scope

Applies to all personnel involved in documented activities within the
SGC through the authorized digital platform.

# Definitions and Acronyms

- **Digital signature** : Signature electronically linked to a person,
  with legal validity and verifiable authenticity.

- **Digital registry** : System that collects, stores and protects staff
  signature data.

- **SGC** : SPIKA Quality Management System.

# Procedure

The system used by SPIKA allows the user who accesses and performs
actions in the environment to be uniquely and unambiguously identified
through their electronic signature. If this electronic signature does
not include the date on which it was issued, it will be necessary to add
it before signing the document.

# Use of digital signature

In official SGC documents (procedures, instructions, records, etc.),
digital mechanisms must be used that allow:

- Uniquely identify the signer (username and password, biometric
  signature, digital certificate, etc.).

- Record the date and time.

- Maintain traceability (through logs or document system).

Signatures can be integrated by:

- Digital signature platforms

- Sign with digital certificate

- Internal access control system with traceability

.
