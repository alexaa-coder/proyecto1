## **1. Target**
The purpose of this procedure is to define the procedure for making purchases at Spika Tech, hereinafter referred to as Spika.
## **2. Outreach**
This procedure applies to all acquisitions made by SPIKA TECH that may have an impact on:
- The quality of the medical product or service
- information security (confidentiality, integrity, availability)
- The operation of the IT systems used in the development, manufacture or provision of
the service.
## 3. Responsibilities and Departments concerned
It is the responsibility of Quality Assurance to supervise compliance with this procedure, a key part of which is the supervision of purchases from duly authorised suppliers.

## **4. Definitions and Acronyms**
- IT (Information Technology): Information Technology
- Critical supplier: Supplier whose product or service may affect regulatory compliance,
patient safety or information security.
- ISO 13485: International Standard for Quality Management Systems for Medical
Devices
- ISO/IEC 27001: International standard for information security management systems
- NDA: Non-Disclosure Agreement
- Security risk: The potential for an asset or service to adversely affect the confidentiality,
integrity or availability of information.
## **5. Procedure** **5.1. Identification of the need**
- Technical description of the good or service
- Justification
- Expected impact (quality, security, IT)
- Functional and technical requirements
## **5.2. Impact and risk assessment**
- A risk analysis is carried out if the good/service is affected:

## **5.3. Supplier selection and evaluation**
The supplier must be previously evaluated according to the supplier evaluation procedure, if it is considered critical for the product developed by SPIKA.
In case of a critical supplier, they should be considered:
- ISO Certifications
- Confidentiality agreements (NDA)
- Security policies
- Compliance history
## **5.4. Request for quotations**
The Purchasing Manager requests a minimum of two quotations. Exceptions justified by availability or specialisation are allowed. IT Department reviews technical requirements and
compatibility. Quality and Information Security review if applicable.
## **5.5. Approval of the acquisition**
Approval is granted by the Purchasing Manager and/or Management, after validation:
- Technical requirements
- Risk assessment
- Regulatory compliance

## **5.6. Reception and verification**
Once the product or service has been received:
- IT performs tests and verifies specifications
- Quality verifies compliance if applicable to the quality system.
- Information Security validates agreed measures (passwords, access, etc.).
## **5.7. Registration and documentation**
All documentation relating to the purchase process, such as the order itself, documents of contracted services, quotations, contracts, offers or receipts, it shall be
be archived within SPIKA TECH's systems in the sharepoint "Management/Finance & Finance".
