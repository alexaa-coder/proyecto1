DATA ANALYSIS Aim
Establish the process to determine, collect, analyze, and interpret the data necessary to demonstrate the suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of Spika Tech's (hereinafter SPIKA) quality management system (QMS), as well as to identify opportunities for improvement based on objective and reliable data.
Scope This procedure applies to all SPIKA areas that generate, use, or analyze data related to QMS performance, including customer satisfaction, product conformity, process performance, suppliers, audits, complaints, and any other relevant sources.
Responsibilities and Departments Affected The affected departments are:
Definitions and Acronyms SGC : SPIKA Quality Management System.
Procedure This procedure describes in a structured manner the steps required to ensure effective analysis of the data that feeds Spika's quality management system (QMS). Its objective is to provide an objective basis for decision-making, continuous improvement, and compliance with established regulatory and internal requirements.
Determining data to be analyzed Internal and external sources of data will be identified to assess the performance of the QMS, including:
Customer feedback (PNT-009).
Product conformity (results of inspections, tests and validations).
Characteristics and trends of the processes ( defined KPIs ).
Results of internal and external audits (PNT-013).
Supplier indicators (deadlines, quality, incidents).
Customer complaints (PNT-007).
Non-conformities and corrective/preventive actions (PNT-004 and PNT-006).
Technical reports for after-sales support or assistance, where applicable .
Data collection Each process must document the data collection methods. The data must be:
Representative and traceable.
Collected in a defined time and manner.
Stored in suitable media (databases, spreadsheets, forms, etc. ).
Data analysis Data analysis will be performed using the monitoring and measurement outputs, as well as any relevant sources, and must include at least the following information:
Feedback Conformity to product requirements
Characteristics and trends of processes, including opportunities for improvement Suppliers
Audits Technical assistance reports, where applicable
As a result of this analysis, if inadequacies arise in the system, this analysis will be the starting point for improvement opportunities.
Distribution of the Procedure Staff members in the departments listed in Section 3 (Responsibilities and Affected Departments) must be provided with a copy of this procedure. In addition, controlled copy No. 1 will be submitted to the Archives for paper archiving.
