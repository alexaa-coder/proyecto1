# Target

The purpose of this procedure is to define the activities to be carried
out to withdraw a product from the market by Spika Tech, hereinafter
SPIKA, quickly and efficiently. This is done if there is real evidence
that the product may constitute a danger to the safety of customers.

# Outreach

The scope of the procedure is all products manufactured by SPIKA.

# Responsibilities and Departments concerned

-   The Management and the Responsible Technician are responsible for
    deciding whether to carry out the withdrawal processes.

-   The Responsible Technician is responsible for:



-   Communicating with the authorities

-   Notify the distribution party of the
    blocking/immobilisation/inactivation/cancellation of access, etc.,
    as the case may be, of the products concerned.

-   Monitor and develop the market recovery process.

# Procedure

All safety alerts received that may have a detrimental impact following
the use of SPIKA products must be evaluated by the Management and the
Responsible Technician. In addition, notifications of complaints that
may be escalated through this procedure may be received.

If the final decision results in the product being blocked, withdrawn
from the market, inaccessible, deactivated, etc., as the case may be, it
shall be carried out as indicated in this procedure.

# Scope of the withdrawal process

Once a decision has been taken to withdraw a product from the market
and/or to disable a product, as appropriate, actions should be swift and
effective, in order to prevent the spread of the risk to the health and
protection of customers and to reduce as far as possible the
consequences that may occur.

Withdrawals may be due to:

-   A complaint or quality defect detected inside the company

-   An external complaint about a quality defect (see **SOP-007
    Complaint Handling**).

-   Requirement of the relevant health authorities.

# Types of withdrawal

# Urgent withdrawal

Where there is a reasonable likelihood that the use of the product may
cause irreparable damage to human health.

In this case, the main objective is to collect and/or disable ALL
products that have gone out of use, including those that may have been
sent to the public, using all necessary measures, including media
publicity if necessary.

# Non-urgent withdrawal

A situation where the use of the product may cause temporary adverse
health effects that are reversible and where the likelihood of
irreversible effects is remote.

In this case the primary objective is the immediate blocking and/or
disabling of all elements of their destinations. Means of communication
shall not be used except in specific high-risk cases.

# Notice to health authorities

Once a decision has been taken to withdraw and/or ban a product or batch
from the market, it is mandatory to inform the Health Authorities
immediately.

The AEMPS or other health entities delegated to SPIKA will be notified
by e-mail and the information provided will be used to assess the
possible risks, with the Responsible Technician being in charge of
making these communications.

# Information needed to send the notice

Following the decision to withdraw a product from the market, it will be
necessary to collect a series of information to be sent to the Health
Authorities, which will generally be as follows:

-   Product batch data (if physical product)

-   Product batch number (if it is a physical product) or product
    version (if it is a software product)

-   Reason for the decision

-   Risk assessment

-   Production data, including:



-   Number of total units produced (if it is a physical product) or
    number of licences sold (if it is a software product)

-   List of customers supplied

-   Name of customers and contact details: telephone, email, postal
    address, etc...

-   Date of distribution/activation of user licence

-   Delivery notes or documentation related to licence
    deliveries/activations.

# Product blocking

It may block/disable products in whole or in part until the
investigation into the reported complaint or defect has been resolved.
The blocking/partial disqualification must be justified or if it has
been possible to narrow down the defect. Preventive
blocking/disqualification may also be carried out and will be lifted if
the defect has been ruled out. The process of blocking/disabling the
software to be carried out remotely if necessary is detailed below:

-   Step 1: Knowing the version of the software to block/disable,
    Spika's IT staff will access the application database, where all
    available versions of the software are stored.

-   Step 2: Once in the database and having identified the version to be
    blocked/disabled, disable it by changing the access parameters that
    are consulted each time the software is started.

-   Step 3: After modifying these parameters, the software version will
    be locked/disabled, preventing the user from accessing that version.

-   Step 4: Once locked/disabled, Spika staff will verify that the user
    is unable to use the software version by running access tests, thus
    concluding the remote software locking process.

# Withdrawal/disqualification report and its effectiveness

Together with the documentation generated by the claim, a report must be
generated where all the traceability of the recovered/disabled units is
recorded.

This report should include as a minimum:

-   Number of units distributed (if it is a physical product) or
    versions affected (if it is a software product)

-   Number of affected units (if a physical product) or affected version
    (if a software product)

-   Number of units recovered/versions disabled, in case of discrepancy
    with the total amounts should be explained and risk assessed.

-   Final risk of withdrawal/disqualification

-   Total customers

-   Clients contacted

-   Batch/version no.

-   Producto afectado

The report should include a conclusion as to whether the
recall/disablement has been deemed effective or not. In addition to the
final reconciliation of units/versions, it should be assessed whether
all customers could be contacted to be informed and that adequate
measures have been taken to proceed with the recall/disablement.

# Simulation

If no withdrawal/disqualification has taken place in the course of a
calendar year, a simulation will be carried out by SPIKA staff in order
to test the effectiveness of the implementation of this procedure and
the methods of communication with customers.
