# Target

The purpose of this procedure is to describe the methodology to be
followed in the initial and ongoing training of Spika Tech, hereinafter
SPIKA, personnel to ensure that all personnel are prepared to perform
their assigned functions.

In the same way, the aim of this procedure is to ensure that all
personnel involved in the production activities of all the production
phases of SPIKA TECH products are adequately trained in the knowledge of
the applicable regulations.

# Outreach

This procedure applies to all personnel involved in SPIKA's production
activities.

#  Responsibilities and Departments concerned

The Management, or the person delegated by it, in collaboration with the
different departments, is responsible for ensuring that staff are
qualified and trained for the correct performance of their duties.

Although no fixed annual training plans are drawn up, the company
maintains a flexible and needs-based approach, organising training
activities as gaps, regulatory, technological or procedural changes are
detected.

Management should provide the necessary resources to carry out such
training when deemed appropriate.

Department heads are responsible for:

-   Detect and communicate the training needs of your team.

-   Organise or coordinate appropriate training actions (internal or
    external).

-   Evaluate the use and effectiveness of the training activities
    carried out.

-   Record staff participation and compliance.

The Responsible Technician, together with the heads of the departments,
must ensure that the training actions carried out are documented and
verified, guaranteeing that the personnel maintain the necessary
competences for the development of their tasks.

# Definitions and Acronyms

-   Training: Period of time during which the worker is being
    prepared to assume the tasks of his job under the responsibility and
    supervision of the trainer.

-   Training Plan: Annual proposal defining both internal and external
    training for employees. These plans are developed by department.

-   Internal Training: Specific training for a specific position,
    which is received in the company itself and given by SPIKA TECH
    personnel. Within this type of training activity are all those
    included in the training procedures for the qualification of
    positions and activities in the different areas of the company. This
    Internal Training must be continuous.

-   External Training: This is training that is obtained from
    outside; it is training for the improvement or optimisation of the
    performance of employees in their jobs and tasks, and is carried out
    by personnel from outside the SPIKA TECH organisation, in its
    facilities or outside them.

-   HR: human resources

-   SOP: Standard Operating Procedure

# Procedure

# Introduction

SPIKA personnel must be qualified and trained for the correct
performance of their duties. Each employee will have an individual
record documenting the training received both on joining and throughout
their time at the company.  
SPIKA does not draw up closed annual training plans, but maintains a
dynamic management based on the training needs detected, organising the
relevant actions when deemed necessary.

# Training plans 

Training actions can be internal or external, face-to-face or
telematic.  
When a training need is detected, whether due to incorporation, change
of position, updating of procedures or continuous improvement, the
employee is informed of the training to be carried out.

Once the training has been completed, the worker must register their
participation and use through a digital form (Google Forms) enabled for
this purpose, which allows traceability and automatic archiving.

# Initial training

Initial training is offered to all new employees. It will include an
introduction to the quality system, the procedures applicable to their
position and a practical part under supervision.

Although no paper forms are used, compliance with this training is
documented on the form provided.

During the initial period, the worker will perform his/her duties under
the supervision of a tutor.

# Continuous training

Continuous training is offered on a regular basis or according to
identified needs. These may be due to:

-   Changes in procedures.

-   Changes in the workplace.

-   Reinstatements after prolonged absences.

-   Continuous improvement or technological evolution.

# Procedural training

When procedures are issued or updated, training is required for the
staff involved. There are three types of training in procedures:

-   Theoretical: requires reading and understanding of the
    procedure, which will be done by reading the corresponding
    controlled copy.

-   Theoretical plus quiz: requires reading and understanding of
    the procedure and passing the related quiz, which must be passed
    with at least 80% of the questions correct.

-   Theoretical-practical: requires reading and understanding of
    the procedure plus practical training in the procedure. In this
    case, the practical training must be carried out before the
    procedure is put into effect. Anyone undergoing initial training who
    comes across a procedure with this type of training marked on it
    should contact the Responsible Technician.

# Courses/training

There are three types of courses or training:

-   Internal: by means of courses and lectures given to employees
    by internal or external SPIKA staff, but always on SPIKA premises.

-   External: by attending courses or conferences outside SPIKA's
    premises.

-   On-line: by attending courses or conferences telematically.

    Registration is always done through the Google form, which allows
    you to collect name, date, type of training, content and
    effectiveness.

# Training evaluation

Whenever possible, at least one questionnaire shall be required in order
to assess the employee's benefit from the training course. This
questionnaire shall be managed by the person delivering the training
course and shall be attached to the training registration form.

# Re-qualification of staff

In the event that the employee spends more than 6 months away from his
or her regular job, training must be arranged upon re-incorporation as
if it were initial training. Staff who remain on the job without being
off work for more than 6 months are considered to be trained through the
continuous training described above.

When a person in the organisation changes jobs, he/she must carry out a
complete re-qualification for the new job as if it were an initial one,
he/she may be exempted from reading and understanding the common
procedures of both jobs, but the specific ones of the new job to be
carried out must be read and understood.

# Archiving of documentation

The Quality Department will be responsible for the management and
custody of the documentation generated from both initial and continuous
training. Therefore, this department must be informed of any change of
job position, and of any personnel joining or leaving the company,

When staff leave the organisation, documentation shall be retained for a
maximum of 5 years, after which it may be destroyed.
