# Target

The aim of this procedure is to define the operations to be followed for
the management of complaints received by Spika Tech, hereinafter SPIKA,
from clients, as well as the management of complaints made to suppliers
so that they can be dealt with and resolved satisfactorily.

# Outreach

The scope of this procedure applies to the handling of all complaints
related to products manufactured at SPIKA and those products or services
that SPIKA purchases or contracts.

#  Responsibilities and Departments concerned

All SPIKA staff may serve as a communicator of a complaint.

The Responsible Technician shall be responsible for managing complaints
from customers, as well as for managing those issued to SPIKA's
suppliers.

All SPIKA departments will be involved in the investigation of
complaints.

# Procedure

# Introduction

A complaint is any expression of dissatisfaction made to an
organisation, in this case SPIKA or suppliers serving SPIKA, regarding
its products or processes, and from which a response or resolution is
explicitly or implicitly expected.

There may be two types of complaints:

-   **Quality claims:** Those where the reported defect could
    potentially have been caused during the manufacturing/conditioning
    process of the product.

-   **Distribution claims**: Those where the reported defect could
    potentially have been caused during the distribution process of the
    product.

# Types of complaints

The following types of complaints may exist depending on their origin:

-   Customer complaints, i.e. customers of SPIKA complaining about a
    defect in products manufactured by SPIKA.

-   Complaints to suppliers, i.e. materials supplied to SPIKA arrive
    with a defect and it is SPIKA that complains to its supplier.

# Classification of complaints

The complaints received are classified according to their
criticality/risk level.

In determining and assigning the level of risk associated with a claim,
the following aspects have to be taken into account:

1.  The severity of the defects detected / reported.

2.  The occurrence of the defect produced.

For this purpose, the following rating scale shall be used as a
reference:








**Severity**
5 = The complaint affects a critical quality attribute or
parameter and/or poses a **serious** health hazard to the
customer.
3 = The complaint concerns a non-critical quality attribute or
parameter and/or poses a **moderate** health hazard to the
customer.
1= The complaint does not affect the quality of the product
**and does not pose a** health hazard to the customer, but
must be documented.




**Occurrence**
5 = Same defect reported for the same product more than
*6* times in the last 12 months.
3 = Same defect reported for the same product between *4 and
6* times *(both inclusive)* within the last 12 months.
1 = Same defect reported for the same product between 0 and 3 times
in the last 12 months.

Once the total risk level associated with the claim has been determined,
the claim will be classified as critical (C), major (M) or minor (m).

In all complaints it will be necessary to investigate the root cause of
the defect detected, however, the level of effort, formality,
documentation to be generated and corrective/preventive action plan
should be proportionate to the risk/criticality of the complaint.

# Critical claim

These are claims for which the total risk level obtained is &gt; 9 (red
zone). These claims must be closed within a maximum period of less than
30 calendar days from the date of receipt of the claim.

# Major claim

These are complaints for which the risk level obtained is &gt; 3 and ≤ 9
(orange area). These claims must be closed within a maximum period of 30
calendar days from the date of receipt of the claim.

# Small claims

These are claims for which the risk level obtained is ≤ 3 (green area).
These claims must be closed within a maximum of 60 calendar days from
the date of receipt of the claim.

# Complaint dismissed

These are those complaints received in which, after 1 month from the
initial communication, it is not possible to obtain the basic data for
their investigation. The basic data are batch number or samples to carry
out the investigation.

# Customer complaint management

This section describes the different phases that form part of the
investigation of quality or distribution claims for products
manufactured at SPIKA.

# Phase I: communication of the complaint 

They may be given in the following ways:

-   Communication from health authorities

-   By sales channel

-   Direct from private individuals, doctors, pharmacists, hospitals,
    etc...

-   Any employee of the company

At this point, when a person receives a complaint, he/she must fill in
the online form to make the communication of the complaint received.

# Phase II: Receipt of the complaint: 

When a complaint is received, the Responsible Technician or the person
he/she delegates will carry out the following actions:

1.  It shall code the claim as follows**: R-XXX/YYY**; where XXX is a
    sequential number and YY is the last two digits of the current year.

2.  It shall proceed to the investigation, where it shall proceed
    according to the nature and extent of the reported defect and the
    information available. The investigation shall include a review of
    the documentation relating to the release.

3.  To confirm the classification of the claim by ruling on whether the
    claim is admissible or not admissible, which is understood as:

    1.  Complaint admissible: if the reported defect is confirmed

    2.  Claim not justified: if, after preliminary investigation, the
        claim cannot be attributed to any of the activities carried out
        under SPIKA's responsibility.

# Phase III: Detailed investigation of the complaint. 

This phase will be completed for those complaints classified as Major or
Critical, where in addition to the Phase II investigation, a more
thorough investigation is carried out to determine the root causes that
led to the defect and to establish an appropriate CAPA Plan. In this
phase all possible points reviewed will be documented in order to
unravel what happened. The provisions of Annex 2 shall be followed.

# Phase IV: Action plan, corrective actions and effectiveness testing

Once the investigation has been completed, actions shall be created in
accordance with SOP-006 **Management of corrective and preventive
actions**. An effectiveness check may be determined in which an
evaluation of the suitability of a corrective or preventive action to
eliminate the root cause of a defect is intended to be made. These
effectiveness checks shall never be a verification that the action has
been implemented.

# Phase V: Closing the complaint

Once the investigation has been completed, the complaint will be closed
with the signature of the Responsible Technician and the knowledge of
the affected department.

A letter or e-mail will be sent to the individuals, doctors, hospitals,
etc... who have made the complaint in order to inform them of the
conclusions and explanation of what happened. A copy of this letter or
e-mail will be kept with the complaint report.

# Archiving of complaints

The Responsible Technician shall ensure that a file is maintained as
described in procedure **PNT-001 Document Management and Archiving** and
a record shall be kept of all complaints processed and received.
