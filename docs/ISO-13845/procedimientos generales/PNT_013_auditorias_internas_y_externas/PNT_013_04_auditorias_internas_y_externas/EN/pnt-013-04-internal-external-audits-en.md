## **1. Aim**
The objective of this procedure is to define the way in which audits are carried out at Spika Tech, hereinafter SPIKA, which are necessary to verify the degree of compliance with the
quality standards and regulations applicable to the departments that make up SPIKA as well as the suppliers that provide a good (product or service) to SPIKA, since the quality of SPIKA's
products depends not only on the organization itself but also on all the materials and services that make up the manufactured product.
_Three types of audits_ are distinguished throughout the procedure :
## ‚Ä¢ GRADE 1: Internal, also known as self-inspections, where SPIKA departments are
audited internally in order to periodically check the efficiency and effectiveness of the Quality Management System.
## ‚Ä¢ GRADE 2: To suppliers: to various suppliers, regardless of the material to be supplied,
as well as to various services, always impacting the quality of the products supplied.
Evaluation questionnaires are also available as annexes to this procedure, through which an initial assessment is conducted to assess compliance with the desired
quality standard.
## ‚Ä¢ GRADE 3: External : those received by organizations, health authorities or official or
private entities, to SPIKA
## **2. Scope**
This procedure applies to all suppliers listed as having a potential impact on product quality and to departments involved in the production and maintenance chain of the Quality
Management System.
## **3. Responsibilities and Departments Affected**
It is the responsibility of all SPIKA staff to collaborate in the execution of internal audits.
It is the responsibility of the Responsible Technician to coordinate the correct execution of the internal and external audit plan, as well as the performance of the audits themselves.

## **4. Definitions and Acronyms** ‚Ä¢ Non-Conformity (NC): An audit finding that does not meet a requirement specified in
an audit criterion.
## ‚Ä¢ Finding: The result of evaluating the audit evidence collected against audit criteria.
Audit findings may indicate either conformity or nonconformity with audit criteria, as well as opportunities for improvement.
## ‚Ä¢ Recommendation: A recommendation for improving quality, safety, or effectiveness.
It does not constitute a deviation.

## **5.2. Grade 1: Internal Audits/Self-Inspections**
The points to be reviewed in each area will depend on the type of activities carried out there.
However, some aspects are common and should be taken into consideration in all areas subject to inspection, as applicable:
## ‚Ä¢ The staff. ‚Ä¢ Premises/ Facilities/ Equipment. ‚Ä¢ Documentation: Standard Operating Procedures and Methods, Specifications,
Manufacturing Guides, etc.
## ‚Ä¢ Organization of the department or area. ‚Ä¢ Validations of procedures. ‚Ä¢ Processes.
It should be noted that self-inspections do not consist of an exhaustive and detailed review of each of the parts and activities that make up a specific department or area, but rather are
based on the evaluation of a representative sample of the inspected process or area, and the deviations detected refer to what was observed.

**5.2.1.** **Frequency**
Self-inspections will be carried out at least once a year, although if necessary due to the severity or number of observations found, the frequency of implementation could be
increased.
**5.2.2.** **Audit staff**
Self-inspections will be conducted by internal personnel unless the Technical Manager decides to subcontract them to a third party external to the organization. Auditing personnel
must be trained in the company's procedures and have completed the qualifications included in Appendix 2. This record must be authorized by the Technical Manager before the audit is
conducted. It will be kept in the Quality Assurance files. The selection of auditors must guarantee objectivity and impartiality in the audited area; therefore, auditors cannot audit their
own work.
## **5.3. Grade 2: To suppliers**
The supplier's impact must be previously assessed, the impact on product quality must be taken into account, therefore at least the following suppliers will be applicable, however, a
recommendation for the evaluations is indicated, but a risk assessment document must be issued to define whether the audit is necessary or not, if such a document does not exist, the
recommendation indicated below will be followed:
## ‚Ä¢ Critical materials that make up the products manufactured at SPIKA. ‚Ä¢ Other materials/services
It is recommended that the initial assessment questionnaires included in the annexes of this procedure be available in order to understand the auditee's quality system, although the
supplier's own documentation may be available.
In cases where an on-site audit of the vendor is not possible (e.g., cloud service providers such as Google, Amazon, or Microsoft, SaaS software providers, or global mission-critical
services providers), a justification for not performing an audit based on the risk analysis will be documented.
- Alternatively, the supplier may be evaluated by:
- Recognized certifications (e.g. ISO 27001, ISO 13485, SOC 2, etc.),
- Publicly available third-party audit reports,

## **_5.4. Grade 3: External_**

_be desirable to receive an agenda from that third party. However, it will be at the discretion of_ _the auditors/inspectors to give advance notice and notify the corresponding visit._

## **5.6. Audit report**
For the final issuance of the Audit Report, the auditing staff will have a maximum of three months from the date of the audit. If any of the nonconformities detected are Critical (C),
these deadlines may be reduced in order to immediately correct the finding.
The reports will be coded as follows:

|INTERNAL AUDIT|EXTERNAL AUDIT|Where:|
|---|---|---|
|AUD -INT-XX/YY|AUD-EXT-XX/YY|XX corresponds to the<br />audit's consecutive<br />number in the current<br />year|
|AUD -INT-XX/YY|AUD-EXT-XX/YY|YY corresponds to the<br />current year of the audit|

The report numbers will be provided in the corresponding database under the custody of the responsible technician or the person delegated by him/her.
