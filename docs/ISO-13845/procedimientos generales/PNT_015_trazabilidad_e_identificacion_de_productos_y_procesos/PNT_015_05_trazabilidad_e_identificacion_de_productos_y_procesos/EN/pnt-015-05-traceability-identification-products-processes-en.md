TRACEABILITY AND IDENTIFICATION OF PRODUCTS AND PROCESSES Target
The purpose of this procedure is to describe how Spika Tech, hereinafter Spika, ensures the traceability of medical devices.
Outreach This procedure applies to all medical devices supplied by SPIKA.
Responsibilities and Departments concerned The operations department shall manage and code the products according to this procedure.

Definitions and Acronyms Medical device (MD): any instrument, device, equipment, software, implant, reagent, material or other article intended by the manufacturer to be used on humans, separately or in combination, for any of the following specific medical purposes:
diagnosis, prevention, monitoring, prediction, prognosis, treatment or alleviation of a disease, diagnosis, follow-up, treatment, relief or compensation for an injury or disability,
investigation, replacement or modification of the anatomy or of a physiological or pathological process or state, information obtained by in vitro examination of samples derived from the human body, including organ, blood and tissue donations, and which does not exert its intended primary action within or on the surface of the human body by pharmacological, immunological or metabolic mechanisms, but to whose function such mechanisms may contribute.
Traceability: refers to the knowledge of the origin of raw materials, the history of the processes applied to the product, the distribution and the location of the product after delivery. A traceability process involves collaboration between the different actors in the supply chain.
Major Change: Corresponds to significant modifications that affect the core functionalities, architecture, design or overall scope of the system/project. This type of change requires a comprehensive approach to ensure quality and compliance.
Change Minor: Includes minor modifications that affect existing functionality, but do not significantly alter the overall behaviour of the system. These changes have a more limited impact and require focused evaluation.
Patch Change: Comprises modifications focused exclusively on correcting specific bugs or problems without altering the functionality of the system or its general behaviour.
Procedure General
Traceability in SPIKA is the guarantee of having a follow-up and control of all the stages of the product from its origins to its distribution to customers, all through established procedures that allow us to know the history, location and trajectory of each product, all supported by computer tools or correct identification of the processes in SPIKA.
Traceability will at all times have the purpose of knowing the status of a product, informing about its origin and giving all the tools to quickly locate a problematic product that has generated complaints from customers or the authorities and to be able to immobilise and even withdraw it from the market when necessary.
Traceability during production SPIKA, due to the type of product it distributes, maintains the traceability of the products during the development of the product in the following way:
It follows a code of one letter followed by 3 numbers. The first number (v1.0.0 - v2.0.0) being a change with significance "MAJOR". The second number (v2.0.0 - .v2.1.0) a change with relevance "MINOR". The third number (v2.1.0 - v2.1.1) are small bugs and fixes "PATCH".
Traceability during distribution During distribution, Spika maintains the serial number and version assigned in production, which are assigned as follows:
It follows a code of one letter followed by 3 numbers. The first number (v1.0.0 - v2.0.0) being a "MAJOR" significant change in the project such as an API change. The second number (v2.0.0 - .v2.1.0) is a change with "MINOR" relevance to the project such as the introduction of a new functionality. The third number (v2.1.0 - v2.1.1) are small bugs and "PATCH" fixes or minor changes such as modifying a line of code for a specific bug.

Identification of product status SPIKA identifies the following software statuses:
Released or in production: in this case the product will be located at https://github.com/SpikaTechSL/VRCardio-VHET360-Visualization/releases.
Obsolete version: an obsolete version is defined as a version with a major version number (1.x.x) lower than the latest released version.
Quarantine or under development in this case the product will be placed under the acronym VRCardio-VHET360-Visualization.
SPIKA identifies the following hardware statuses:
Released or in production: the product will be marked with a green label.
Rejected or obsolete version: the product will be marked with an orange label.
Quarantine or under development: the product shall be marked with a red label.
Tagged UDI Assignment of UDI: The software developed by SPIKA will be assigned a Unique Identifier (UDI) in accordance with the provisions of Regulation (EU) 2017/745 of the European Parliament and of the Council, at the time the product is finalised and released for market availability.
Application of the UDI: The UDI must be applied in a clear and legible manner on each product and, where necessary, on its packaging, using the technical standards specified in the regulation.
EUDAMED registration: Before placing the product on the market, the UDI of the product must be registered in the EU's EUDAMED Database, ensuring that all relevant information is complete and up to date.
Updating and maintenance: It is SPIKA's responsibility to keep the information associated with the UDI registered with EUDAMED up to date, including any relevant changes to the product information or the UDI itself.
