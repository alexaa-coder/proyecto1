MANAGEMENT OF CORRECTIVE AND PREVENTIVE ACTIONS Aim
The objective of this procedure is to define the management of corrective and preventive actions at Spika Tech, hereinafter SPIKA, which will result in nonconformities, complaints or improvement opportunities, as well as any system records or feedback.
Scope This procedure applies to all SPIKA staff with regard to action management. This procedure establishes guidelines for managing these actions, as well as their monitoring and implementation.
Responsibilities and Departments Affected All SPIKA personnel are responsible for carrying out the activities assigned to them to correct or prevent non-conformities.
It is the responsibility of the Responsible Technician or the person he delegates to monitor the implementation of corrective and preventive measures .
The affected departments are those indicated in the following table:
Definitions and Acronyms Root cause : is the final cause that if corrected would prevent the recurrence of the event.
Corrective action : Action to eliminate the root cause of a detected nonconformity or other undesirable situation.
Preventive action : action to eliminate the possible root cause of a potential nonconformity Correction : Action taken to eliminate the condition of nonconformity/deviation from the affected product or system.
PNT: Standard Operating Procedure CAPA: from English, Corrective Action Preventive Action , in Spanish, corrective action preventive action.
Procedure Introduction
CAPA refers to the activities or actions to be carried out after a nonconformity occurs or to prevent a nonconformity, corrective and preventive, respectively. These actions will have a responsible person and assigned department, as well as a deadline, which will have been previously established by reviewing the records that led to them. Therefore, all staff, in their commitment to quality, are responsible and committed to completing them on time. Should there be any obstacles, extensions to the initially agreed-upon dates may be arranged.
Assignment of CAPAs When non-conformities are opened in SPIKA, they may have different origins, such as:
Non-conformities Claims
Internal audits External audits
Inspections or audits carried out at SPIKA Results or conclusions of quality management system review reports
Result of any report prepared These activities will be carried out to prevent recurrences or potential nonconformities. Therefore, when a SPIKA employee prepares a document capable of determining the root cause of a nonconformity, they must request the assignment of a CAPA number to the Technical Department.
CAPAs may be composed of one or more actions to be performed, so that specific actions are always available for each of the root causes found in a nonconformity.
Coding the LAYERS There will be a database maintained by the Technical Department that will have the following content:
CAPA No.
Origin of the action, the associated records or documents must be indicated here Description of the action
Head of Execution Execution Department
Deadline for execution Extension of the action, if any, first and second extension
New deadline, first and second date The database format is attached in Annex 1 of this procedure.
Each action will be coded as follows:
Corrective Action: 		AC-XXX/YY Preventive action: 		AP-XXX/YY
Extension : PR-XXX/YY Where, XXX will be a 3-digit consecutive number and YY the last two digits of the current year.
Adding a LAYER Once the necessary investigations or reports have been completed, actions may be taken to prevent nonconformities or other events from recurring. Therefore, the opening of preventive or corrective actions will be managed, according to Annex 2 of the CAPA Template . This form will be duly signed by the person opening the action and submitted to the Technical Department, which will review the content, assign the code, and enter the action into the database for follow-up.
Action tracking Every six months, the Technical Manager or the person designated by him/her will review the list included in the database and will send the necessary written reminders to the departments that have assigned actions that are close to completion.
Copies of CAPA forms may also be made to the responsible staff so that they are familiar with their content and details, if necessary.
Date extension People with assigned CAPAs are responsible for completing them by the deadline; however, on certain occasions, due to impediments, extensions of the date may be requested using the corresponding extension form, which can be found under Annex 3 CAPA Date Extension .
Distribution of the Procedure Staff members in the departments listed in Section 3 (Responsibilities and Affected Departments) must be provided with a copy of this procedure. In addition, controlled copy No. 1 will be submitted to the Archives for paper archiving.
