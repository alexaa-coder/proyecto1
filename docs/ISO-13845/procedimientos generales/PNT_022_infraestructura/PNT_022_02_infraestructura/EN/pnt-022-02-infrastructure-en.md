## **4. Definitions and Acronyms** • Medical device: any instrument, device, equipment, software, implant, reagent, material
or other article intended by the manufacturer to be used on humans, separately or in combination, for any of the following specific medical purposes:
- diagnosis, prevention, monitoring, prediction, prognosis, treatment or relief of a
disease,
- diagnosis, follow-up, treatment, relief or compensation for an injury or disability,
- Research, replacement or modification of anatomy or of a physiological or
pathological process or state, obtaining information by means of in vitro examination of samples from the human body, including organ, blood and tissue
donations, and which does not perform its principal intended action inside or on the surface of the human body by pharmacological, immunological or metabolic
mechanisms, but to whose function such mechanisms may contribute .
## **5. Procedure**
SPIKA is located on the campus of the Rey Juan Carlos University (https://www.urjc.es/). The campus has a library and several teaching and research laboratories, as well as the
Technology Support Center and the University Rectorate. The campus is 280 square meters.
At this headquarters located in Madrid, the software is developed using a storage systems architecture on the GitHub electronic platform (https://github.com/)
Number of servers: 38 portable servers located at SpikaTech's offices on the Rey Juan Carlos University campus in Móstoles (except for remote workers or those based outside of Rey
Juan Carlos University, whose servers are hosted at their off-site workplace).
Type of connections/services: All types of connections are made wirelessly or via Wi-Fi connection cable.
Backups: All data available from the vr-cardio application is backed up to a database located [in Firebase ( https://firebase.google.com/?hl=es-419 )](https://firebase.google.com/?hl=es-419)
