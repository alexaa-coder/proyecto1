## **1. Aim**
The objective of this procedure is the procedure for the selection, evaluation, classification, approval and re-evaluation of suppliers that supply products or services that affect the quality
of the medical device, ensuring their compliance with the requirements of the organization and the applicable regulations.
## **2. Scope**
The scope of this procedure applies to all third-party providers that supply products or services used in the development, validation, maintenance, or support of the product
developed by SPIKA.
## 3. Responsibilities and Departments Affected
The Technical Manager or the person to whom he/she delegates will approve or reject the evaluation of the suppliers and will safeguard all the information and documentation
generated.

## **4. Definitions and Acronyms**
- Software : A set of instructions, programs, or applications designed to perform specific
tasks within a computer system.
- Software vendor : An external entity responsible for providing software or software
components that may be integrated, used, or required in the development, validation, or maintenance of products or services.
- Cloud computing : A technology service delivery model that enables remote access,
via networks, to shared and configurable computing resources (such as storage, servers, databases, applications, and services).
- Cloud provider : An organization that offers cloud-based services (such as AWS,
Microsoft Azure, Google Cloud).
## **5. Procedure** **5.1. Introduction**
Having approved suppliers is essential to meet customer, regulatory, and organizational requirements.
Furthermore, having approved suppliers prevents quality issues associated with the products or services supplied, as well as ensuring the search for alternative suppliers to ensure supply.
## **5.2. Selection**
Anyone within the organization can propose new suppliers by completing a structured form.
This form collects all the information necessary to evaluate the supplier's suitability, including the materials or services offered, associated risks, and selection criteria.

|Type of provider|Material|
|---|---|

|Uncritical|Supplier that does not directly affect the characteristics or<br />performance of the product|
|Not evaluable|Large technology companies or government agencies<br />whose processes are not available for direct evaluation,<br />but whose services are essential.|

## **5.4. Initial evaluation**
Critical suppliers are required:
- Mandatory documentation :
`o` ISO 13485 (preferred) or ISO 9001 certification or other relevant certification (SOC 2, ISO 27001, etc.).
`o` Signed quality agreement (Annex 1), NDA or documented acceptance of general conditions.
- Technical evaluation :
`o` Assessment of the impact on the safety, integrity and performance of the medical device.

- Audit :
`o` If feasible, a remote or in-person audit will be conducted.
If the supplier does not have a quality certificate :
- Its technical reliability will be assessed through tests, internal trials, references and
experience.
- The justification for acceptance will be documented in Annex 2.
If the supplier does not accept audit :
- It will be documented as "Non-audited Supplier".
- Reinforced controls will be established, such as service validation, redundancy, and
frequent integration tests .
Non-critical suppliers are required:
- Basic registration in Annex 2.
- Evaluation of previous history if you have already collaborated.
- No quality certificate or audit is required.
Non-evaluable suppliers must:
- Document in Annex 2 as “Not evaluable”.
- Attach general terms of service, security and privacy policies, and available public
certifications (e.g., ISO 27001, SOC 2, CSA STAR, etc.).
- Compensatory internal control is applied:
`o` Internal functional validations.
`o` Continuous service monitoring.
`o` Specific risk analysis.

## **5.5. Supplier approval**
The Technical Manager or the person designated by him or her is responsible for issuing a decision on the approval or rejection of an evaluated supplier. This decision will be made after
reviewing the documentation received from the supplier, signing and dating the approval or rejection. The supplier list will then be updated; a sample/template is included in Annex 3 .
A quality agreement template is proposed in Annex 1, although agreements submitted by the supplier may be signed or modifications made to the one included in this procedure.
All generated documentation will be filed and safeguarded by the Responsible Technician.

1. This quality agreement covers all aspects of quality related to the manufacturing, pack aging, distribution, and storage of products, and establishes and defines the obligations and responsibilities of both parties in relation to the quality standard required for
the products to be supplied by the supplier.
2. This agreement is not intended to define legal or commercial relationships .
Article 1: Certificate of Analysis or Certificate of Conformity 1. The buyer is required to have a certificate of analysis for the products to be purchased.
2. The certificate of analysis must be provided with each delivery of material, if required by the type of product to be supplied.
3. The certificate of analysis must contain at least the following information:
a. Name and exact address of the supplier. If the supplier and manufacturer are different, provide the manufacturer's exact address.
b. Product name c. Product batch number
d. Reference to specifications e. Tests carried out in accordance with applicable regulations
f. Release date and signature of qualified personnel g. In Spanish or English.
Article 2: Exchange Control 1. The supplier must have a change control system in place.
2. The supplier must inform the buyer of any changes made to the product, especially those that impact the quality of the supplied product, so that the buyer can analyze
their impact .
3. The implementation of major changes must have the buyer's approval before being executed.
4. The supplier will provide the material in compliance with applicable standards and regulations and will provide the necessary documentation of those changes to meet the requirements of health regulatory agencies, if applicable.
5. The supplier shall notify and inform the buyer within a reasonable period of time.

Article 3: Audits 1. The supplier is open to on-site audits and/or postal/documentary/remote audits. The
supplier must allow access to the buyer's premises, as well as the required documentation, without interfering with ongoing operations .
2. The results of the audits and observations will be submitted by the buyer in writing. The supplier will ensure follow-up and implementation of any required corrective measures.
3. The frequency of audits will depend on the results of the audits and the quality of the supplier. If there are no critical nonconformities regarding product quality, the frequency will not be more than every three years.
Article 4. Retention samples 1. The supplier will keep sufficient retention samples to repeat analysis at least twice
against specifications.
Article 5: Documentation 1. The supplier will store the production documentation and all original documentation
related to the product, which will be protected and with controlled access.
2. The original documentation is accessible in the event that the buyer attends the facili ties for an audit.
Article 6. Claims/Complaints 1. The supplier will respond to complaints submitted by the buyer within a period of no
more than 20 business days. In the case of serious complaints, the maximum response time will be 5 days, in order to deliver a written preliminary report and a final report
within 10 days.
2. In the event of non-compliance with the indicated deadlines, the supplier may provide interim reports to the buyer.
3. The supplier will inform the buyer if it receives serious complaints with a high impact on the products supplied to the buyer.

Article 7. Withdrawal 1. In the event of a recall by the supplier, the buyer will be immediately informed of the
quality defects.
2. In the event of a recall, the buyer and supplier will agree on and decide on roles and responsibilities, as well as the coordination of the investigation.
3. The buyer is the one who will make the final decision on the finished product based on scientific criteria and risk management.
Article 8: Raw materials 1. The supplier is responsible for purchasing, storing, analyzing, and disposing of raw ma
terials, as well as ensuring that each one meets current requirements.
Article 9. Deviations 1. In the event of serious quality-related incidents detected after shipment to the buyer,
the supplier must inform the buyer as soon as possible.
Article 10. Packaging and labeling.
1. Appearance: The labeling of the product supplied by the supplier must be identifiable, located in a visible location and in adequate dimensions for human reading.
2. Contents: The product must be labeled with the product description, product name, batch number, supplier name, and quantity.
Article 11. Regulatory documentation 1. The supplier will be responsible for preparing the regulatory documentation required
by the buyer and keeping it updated according to requirements.
