## **1. Aim**
The objective of this procedure is the system for selecting, evaluating and approving Spika Tech suppliers, hereinafter SPIKA, establishing the basic requirements for approving
suppliers that lead to the affirmation that:
## • Approved suppliers are available who can guarantee the supply of products or the
performance of activities that are to be subcontracted.
## • Suppliers have the necessary requirements to provide a material/service with the
required quality, in accordance with internal specifications and regulatory and customer requirements.
## **2. Scope**
The scope of this procedure applies to the selection, evaluation and approval of suppliers, as follows:

|Supplier|Material|
|---|---|
|Manufacturers|Hardware components, external devices|
|Software providers|Cloud platforms, embedded software|
|Service providers|Validation, qualification, software testing, quality control,<br />consulting|
|Logistics providers|Transportation, distribution or warehouse management<br />services|

## 3. Responsibilities and Departments Affected
The Sales Department, in coordination with Management or its delegate, is responsible for contacting suppliers to request information on the evaluation of this procedure, as well as
informing the Technical Manager of the need to evaluate new suppliers.

## **5. Procedure** **5.1. Introduction**
Having approved suppliers is essential to meet customer, regulatory, and organizational requirements.
Furthermore, having approved suppliers prevents quality issues associated with the products or services supplied, as well as ensuring the search for alternative suppliers to ensure supply.
## **5.2. Selection**
Anyone within the organization can propose new suppliers by completing a structured form.
This form collects all the information necessary to evaluate the supplier's suitability, including the materials or services offered, associated risks, and selection criteria.
Suppliers with a direct supply chain and those with satisfactory prior experience will be prioritized. The Quality Department will be responsible for reviewing and validating the
information received before proceeding with the formal supplier evaluation.
## **5.3. Assessment**
Suppliers may be classified as critical or non-critical depending on their impact on:
1. The quality management system : suppliers whose products, materials or services are essential to meet regulatory and quality requirements.
2. Processes and activities : suppliers that directly affect the manufacturing or servicing of the final product

3. The end product : Suppliers that provide key components or services that impact the safety, efficacy, or regulatory compliance of the product.
Criteria for determining critical suppliers:
- Product or material manufacturers: Those who supply raw materials, key components,
or packaging materials that have a direct impact on product compliance with regulatory requirements.
- Service providers: external consultants or services that affect validated or critical
processes.
- Distributors: If they act as intermediaries for products that have a direct impact on final
product requirements or regulatory compliance.
Evaluation system according to the type and impact of the supplier Critical Suppliers:
A more thorough assessment is required due to the direct impact on product and process quality. The documentation to be collected includes:
- Quality Agreement (See Annex 1): Defines the responsibilities and expectations
between the parties.
- Quality certifications: ISO, GMP, GDP or other applicable standards.
- Technical specifications or product sheets (if applicable): Guarantees that the product
meets the established requirements.
- Declarations or additional documentation : Declarations of conformity, audit reports,
quality tests, or other documents specific to the product or service.
- Audits (if applicable): Conduct on-site or remote audits to verify compliance with
quality standards and established agreements.
Non-critical suppliers:
For suppliers with a lower impact, a proportional assessment will be applied, focusing on basic documentation:
- Quality Agreement (See Annex 1).
- Quality certifications available.

## **5.4. Supplier approval**
The Technical Manager or the person designated by them is responsible for issuing a decision on the approval or rejection of an evaluated supplier. They will issue their decision after
reviewing the documentation received from the supplier and preparing the form in Annex 2, signing and dating the approval or rejection. The supplier list will then be updated; a
sample/template is included in Annex 3 .
A quality agreement template is proposed in Annex 1, although agreements submitted by the supplier may be signed or modifications made to the one included in this procedure.
All generated documentation will be filed and safeguarded by the Responsible Technician.
## **5.5. Re-evaluation of suppliers**
Suppliers may be re-evaluated if:
**-** After inactivity of at least 5 years and they are required to apply again
**-** According to the history of complaints or incidents arising from them, to be concluded
and reviewed in the Quality Management System Review Report by Management.
## **5.6. Audits**
In cases where the critical nature of the service provided and after evaluating the available documentation, an audit will be conducted at the supplier's facilities. The provisions of PNT013, Internal and External Audits, will be followed.

3. The implementation of major changes must have the buyer's approval before being executed.
4. The supplier will provide the material in compliance with applicable standards and regulations and will provide the necessary documentation of those changes to meet the requirements of health regulatory agencies, if applicable.
5. The supplier shall notify and inform the buyer within a reasonable period of time.

Article 3: Audits 1. The supplier is open to on-site audits and/or postal/documentary/remote audits. The
supplier must allow access to the buyer's premises, as well as the required documentation, without interfering with ongoing operations .
2. The results of the audits and observations will be submitted by the buyer in writing. The supplier will ensure follow-up and implementation of any required corrective measures.
3. The frequency of audits will depend on the results of the audits and the quality of the supplier. If there are no critical nonconformities regarding product quality, the frequency will not be more than every three years.
Article 4. Retention samples 1. The supplier will keep sufficient retention samples to repeat analysis at least twice
against specifications.
Article 5: Documentation 1. The supplier will store the production documentation and all original documentation
related to the product, which will be protected and with controlled access.
2. The original documentation is accessible in the event that the buyer attends the facili ties for an audit.
Article 6. Claims/Complaints 1. The supplier will respond to complaints submitted by the buyer within a period of no
more than 20 business days. In the case of serious complaints, the maximum response time will be 5 days, in order to deliver a written preliminary report and a final report
within 10 days.
2. In the event of non-compliance with the indicated deadlines, the supplier may provide interim reports to the buyer.

Article 7. Withdrawal 1. In the event of a recall by the supplier, the buyer will be immediately informed of the
quality defects.
2. In the event of a recall, the buyer and supplier will agree on and decide on roles and responsibilities, as well as the coordination of the investigation.

Article 9. Deviations 1. In the event of serious quality-related incidents detected after shipment to the buyer,
the supplier must inform the buyer as soon as possible.
Article 10. Packaging and labeling.
1. Appearance: The labeling of the product supplied by the supplier must be identifiable, located in a visible location and in adequate dimensions for human reading.
2. Contents: The product must be labeled with the product description, product name, batch number, supplier name, and quantity.
Article 11. Regulatory documentation 1. The supplier will be responsible for preparing the regulatory documentation required
by the buyer and keeping it updated according to requirements.
