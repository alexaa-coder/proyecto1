**Finding No 1**




**Description of nonconformity**


The internal audit report lacks adequate process
coverage and objective evidence :

Only one person (Quality Director) was audited, limiting
verification of QMS implementation across functions. Furthermore, key
processes were not audited, including supplier control, complaint
handling, software validation, and internal audits.
The report does not provide adequate objective evidence (e.g.,
sampled records, checklists) to support conformity claims.
The report and the agenda do not have identification as part of
the QMS system



**Requirement**


ISO 13485:2016  8.2.4
MDR Annex IX 2.2


**Objective evidence of
nonconformity**


Internal Audit report


**Classification**


☐ major NC
☒ minor NC
☐ observation


Auditor VC
Date 15/7/2025


**Client response**
**Date:**


**Root cause analysis**


The internal audit was carried out by only one person,
which limited the coverage and objectivity of the process. Furthermore,
due to partial planning, some relevant processes were not included. The
absence of objective evidence and the lack of formal identification in
the documentation are due to insufficient application of the current
audit procedure and the use of outdated templates.


**Correction(s) to eliminate the
nonconformity**


Record the non-conformity in the quality system and
provide traceability of the follow-up.


**Corrective action(s) to eliminate the cause of
the nonconformity**


Review and update the internal audit procedure
to:
• Include minimum criteria for process coverage and evidence
sampling, requiring all departments affected by the QMS to be
audited.
• Highlight the need to use controlled templates with identification
in accordance with the document management system.
• Ensure that future internal audits adequately plan the scope and
provide for the review of key processes.

**Finding No 2**




**Description of nonconformity**


The change handling SOP does not address impacts on MDR
compliance, risk management, UDI-DI, or reporting to the Notified
Body.


**Requirement**


MDR Annex IX 2.2, 2.4


**Objective evidence of
nonconformity**


PNT-005


**Classification**


☐ major NC
☒ minor NC
☐ observation


Auditor PM
Date 15/7/2025


**Client response**
**Date:**


**Root cause analysis**


The change management procedure does not explicitly
address the assessment of the impact on compliance with the MDR, nor on
critical elements such as risk management, UDI-DI or the need for
notification to the Notified Body. This omission is due to the fact that
the procedure was initially adapted from a generic model without
integrating all the specific requirements applicable to medical devices
under the MDR, especially at an early stage of quality system
development.


**Correction(s) to eliminate the
nonconformity**


Record the non-conformity in the quality system and
provide traceability of the follow-up.


**Corrective action(s) to eliminate the cause of
the nonconformity**


Review and update the change control procedure
to:
• Include a specific section on regulatory impact assessment
(MDR).
• Add fields in the change request template to record the risk
management impact analysis, UDI identifiers, technical documentation and
the need for notification to HTCert.
• Establish a checkpoint to review whether the change affects
information already assessed by the Notified Body.

**Finding No 3**




**Description of nonconformity**


The methodology used in the FMEA report for calculating
the Risk Priority Number (RPN) is based on the multiplication of
severity, probability, and detectability and does not align with the
Risk Management procedure PNT-002.04, which defines the RPN calculation
as the product of severity and probability only


**Requirement**


ISO13485 4.1.2b
MDR Annex IX 2.2


**Objective evidence of
nonconformity**


MR-001.01_24
PNT-002


**Classification**


☐ major NC
☒ minor NC
☐ observation


Auditor VC
Date 15/7/2025


**Client response**
**Date:**


**Root cause analysis**


A discrepancy has been identified between the
methodology applied in the FMEA report and the risk management procedure
PNT-002.04. While the procedure defines the calculation of the RPN as
the multiplication of severity and probability (RPN = S × P), the FMEA
report uses the traditional formula S × P × D (detectability). This
inconsistency is due to the lack of alignment between the template used
for the FMEA and the internal criteria defined in the procedure.


**Correction(s) to eliminate the
nonconformity**


Record the non-conformity in the quality system and
provide traceability of the follow-up.


**Corrective action(s) to eliminate the cause of
the nonconformity**


• Updating the standard FMEA analysis template to
correctly reflect the internal methodology for calculating the RPN (S ×
P).
• Review of previous risk analyses to identify possible inconsistent
documents.

**Finding No 4**




**Description of nonconformity**


The issues related with the UDI (basic udi-di, udi-pi
etc) are not clearly addressed in the QMS. (E.g. It is not covered the
impact of versioning to the udi-pi, or the impact of change of the
intended purpose of the product to the basic udi-di)


**Requirement**


MDR *Article 27*


**Objective evidence of
nonconformity**


PNT-019 LABELING MANAGEMENT


**Classification**


☐ major NC
☒ minor NC
☐ observation


Auditor PM
Date 15/7/2025


**Client response**
**Date:**


**Root cause analysis**


The lack of clear development on UDI-related aspects
(including Basic UDI-DI, UDI-DI and UDI-PI) within the quality
management system is due to a limited initial interpretation of the MDR
requirements in this area. In particular, the impacts of certain changes
on the unique identifier, such as changes in the version/revision of the
software (UDI-PI) or changes in the intended use of the product (Basic
UDI-DI), are not adequately addressed.


**Correction(s) to eliminate the
nonconformity**


Record the non-conformity in the quality system and
provide traceability of the follow-up.


**Corrective action(s) to eliminate the cause of
the nonconformity**


Modification of the labelling control procedure to
include:
• Clear definition of UDI-DI, UDI-PI and Basic UDI-DI elements
• Records and traceability of UDI code assignment.
• Specification of the person responsible for generating, maintaining
and communicating UDI codes.
Update of the change control procedure to:
• Include UDI impact analysis for every modification.
• Document when a release generates a new UDI-PI.
• Include criteria for determining when a change affects the Basic
UDI-DI (e.g. change in intended use or product family).

**Finding No 5**




**Description of nonconformity**


The SOP PNT-030 for Strategy for regulatory compliance
does not clearly describes the identification of relevant legal
requirements, the identification of the sources as well as the
responsibilities for the tasks. In addition the follow up of CS and/or
harmonized standards is not covered


**Requirement**


MDR Annex IX 2.2


**Objective evidence of
nonconformity**


PNT-030 REGULATORY MONITORING


**Classification**


☐ major NC
☒ minor NC
☐ observation


Auditor PM
Date 15/7/2025


**Client response**
**Date:**


**Root cause analysis**


Procedure PNT-030 does not explicitly and in a
structured way address the mechanisms to:
• Identify applicable legal requirements (MDR, national legislation,
etc.).
• Determine the official sources of information (EU Official Journal,
European Commission website, notified bodies, industry
associations).
• Assign clear responsibilities for monitoring these
requirements.
• Monitor changes in Common Specifications (CS) or relevant
harmonised standards.
• This omission is due to the fact that the procedure was initially
drafted early in the development of the system, and was not revised
after the full entry into force of the MDR.


**Correction(s) to eliminate the
nonconformity**


Record the non-conformity in the quality system and
provide traceability of the follow-up.


**Corrective action(s) to eliminate the cause of
the nonconformity**


Revision of procedure PNT-030, including:
• Specific section for the identification of applicable legal and
regulatory requirements, including MDR, applicable directives, national
legislation, and MDCG guidelines.
• Inclusion of a list of official sources of updated information
(websites, bulletins, standards databases, etc.).
• Clear assignment of responsible parties for monitoring, reviewing
and updating these requirements.
• Description of the monitoring process for harmonised standards and
Common Specifications (CS), including frequency, monitoring tools (e.g.
CEN notifications, OJEU) and criteria for assessing their
applicability.
• Integration with the risk assessment and change management
procedure to ensure that the system is updated when requirements
change.
