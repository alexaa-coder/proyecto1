# **REQUIEREMENT SPECIFICATION**
**Electrodes and Distribution**
- The electrodes selected during our testing must be used, ensuring that the
chosen material is optimal for the defined use case.
- Uniform distribution of electrodes across the torso:
`o` **30 electrodes** strategically placed to capture signals in the required positions.
`o` **4 references** positioned to maintain constant contact with the patient’s torso.
**Signal Conduction**
- Cables must adequately conduct signals from the electrodes to the connector
without compromising signal quality or distribution.
- Cable material: resistant to clinical use, flexible, and compatible with the
characteristics of the captured signals.
**Connector and Electronics**
- The design must include a **single connector** that consolidates all cable
connections and enables direct integration with the external electronics.
- The connector must be robust, reliable, and easy to connect and disconnect.
**Support for Electronics**
- Include a **secure and accessible pocket** to house the electronics, with
- The vest must comply with applicable safety and regulatory standards for
medical devices, including:
`o` **Electrical safety** `o` **Electromagnetic compatibility (EMC)**
`o` **Chemical characterisation** `o` **Biocompatibility**
**Design and Sizing**
- Provide an ergonomic design that is comfortable for patients during clinical use.
- Initial prototype in a **standard size**, with provisions for larger sizes in future
production phases.
**Maintenance and Reuse**
- The vest must be **washable and reusable in a clinical setting**, after the
electronics are removed.
- Materials should be durable and resistant to washing cycles according to
hospital protocols.
