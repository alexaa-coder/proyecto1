# **1. ANNEX 1: Model Quality Agreement**
1. The buyer is required to have a certificate of analysis of the products to be purchased.
2. The certificate of analysis must be provided with each delivery of material, if required by the type of product to be supplied.
3. The certificate of analysis must contain at least the following information:
a. Name and exact address of the supplier. If the supplier and the manufacturer are different, please indicate the exact address of
the manufacturer.
b. Product name c. Product batch number
d. Reference to specifications e. Tests carried out in accordance with applicable regulations
f. Date of release and signature of the personnel qualified to do so g. In Spanish or English.
Article 2: Change Control 1. The supplier must have a change control system in place.
2. The supplier must inform the buyer of changes made to the product, especially those that have an impact on the quality of the delivered product,
in order for the buyer to analyse the impact of these changes.
3. The implementation of major changes must be approved by the purchaser prior to implementation.
4. The supplier shall provide the material under the applicable rules and regulations and shall provide the necessary documentation of those
changes to complete requirements of health regulatory agencies if applicable.
5. The supplier shall notify and inform the buyer within a reasonable period of time.
Article 3: Audits 1. The supplier is open to on-site audits and/or postal/documentary/remote
audits. The supplier must allow entry to the buyer's premises, as well as to the required documentation, without interfering with the operations being
carried out.
2. The result of the audits and the observations shall be forwarded by the purchaser in writing. The supplier shall ensure the follow-up and
implementation of the required corrective measures.
3. The frequency of audits will depend on the outcome of the audits and the quality of the supplier. If there are no critical non-conformities on the quality
of the products, the frequency shall not be more than every 3 years.
Article 4. Retention samples 1. The supplier shall keep sufficient retention samples to repeat analyses at
least 2 times against specifications.
Article 5: Documentation 1. The supplier shall store the production documentation and all original
documentation related to the product, which shall be protected and with controlled access.
2. The original documentation is accessible in the event that the purchaser attends the premises for an audit.
Article 6. Complaints/complaints 1. The supplier shall respond to complaints issued by the purchaser within 20
working days, in the case of serious complaints the maximum response time shall be 5 days to deliver a written pre-report and a final report within
10 days.
2. In the event of non-compliance with the deadlines indicated, the supplier may provide interim reports to the purchaser.
3. The supplier shall inform the buyer if he receives serious complaints with high impact on the products supplied to the buyer.
Article 7. Withdrawal 1. In the event of a recall by the supplier, the buyer shall be informed
immediately of the quality defects.
2. In case of a recall the buyer and the supplier will agree and decide on the roles and responsibilities as well as the coordination of the investigation. In
the event of disagreement, duties and responsibilities shall be attributed in accordance with the contract and the regulations in force.
3. It is the buyer who will make the final decision on the finished product based on scientific criteria and risk management.
Article 9: Raw materials 1. The supplier is responsible for purchasing, storing, analysing and releasing
the raw materials and ensuring that each raw material meets the current requirements.
Article 10. Deviations 1. In case of serious quality incidents detected after shipment to the buyer, the
supplier must inform the buyer as soon as possible.
Article 11. Packaging and labelling.
1. Appearance: the labelling of the product supplied by the supplier must identify the product, in a visible place and in a size suitable for reading by
people.
2. Content: the product must be labelled with the product description, product name, batch number, supplier's name and quantity.
3. The regulatory requirements for packaging and labelling, if any, shall also be complied with.
Article 12. Regulatory documentation 1. The supplier shall be responsible for preparing the regulatory
documentation required by the purchaser and keeping it updated as required.
Article 13. Product release 1. The supplier shall not supply product to the buyer until it is released by the
supplier. Quarantined shipments may be accepted with the prior approval of the buyer.
Article 14. Specifications 1. The supplier will release the product according to the specifications and
tests agreed by both parties, buyer and supplier.
Article 15. Confidentiality 1. The supplier and the buyer understand that any information of a confidential
nature provided by either party is to be treated in strict confidence.
