# Introduction​

The management of Spika Tech, hereinafter SPIKA, has decided to adopt
the quality management system proposed in the current version of ISO
13485 as a strategic decision for the organization to become part of the
life cycle of the medical devices it supplies.

The Quality System developed by our company takes into account various
factors and needs, such as the specific objectives of our business
activity, the legal or regulatory specifics of the products we
manufacture, and all of this in line with the processes and resources
employed, as well as the size and structure of our organization.

By applying and complying with this standard, we aim to demonstrate our
ability to meet the requirements established by our customers,
applicable regulations, and those established by the organization
itself, with the goal of obtaining medical devices with the expected
quality and achieving customer satisfaction with the product supplied.

For this reason, SPIKA has adopted a process management approach to
quality management from the very beginning. Any input element of the
processes is transformed into elements of subsequent processes,
establishing interrelationships between them. This approach will allow
for improved control over activities.

The Quality System that the organization has decided to implement will
be a living entity that will feed over time with the experience acquired
and will allow SPIKA to:

-   Understand and comply with the requirements

-   Consider processes in terms of added value

-   Obtain results from the performance and effectiveness of processes

-   Continuously improve processes objectively.

development, we are leading advances in various fields such as virtual
reality, robotics, and artificial intelligence, as evidenced by our
awards and recognition. We always focus on our research base and
innovation.

SPIKA is a company operating in the technology sector. It is committed

combines design and programming processes, utilizing the latest
techniques. It strives for quality in its development.

Our professionals are a team of highly qualified professionals, but most

# Mission

Our mission is to make a difference, to be the organization where
customers find the tools they need to perform their personal and
professional activities with distinction, committed to a new future
through the development of new diagnostic techniques and innovative
tools that address unmet medical needs.

SPIKA has developed VR-CARDIO, a pioneering technology that offers 360°
stereoscopic, holographic, and three-dimensional visualization, allowing
the noninvasive visualization of the heart's electrical signals.
VR-CARDIO aims to improve the diagnosis and prevention of cardiovascular
diseases, which are the leading cause of death in our country. This
innovation represents a significant advance in the fight against cardiac
arrhythmias. As a result, it was recognized as one of the projects
selected for the EIC Accelerator program. VR-CARDIO has also received
several national and international awards and recognitions, and several
of the country's leading cardiologists have recognized its potential
application in clinical practice specializing in the treatment of
arrhythmias.

# Scope

This quality manual developed by SPIKA is for the Design and Development
of medical devices (software and data acquisition systems for
cardiological medical use)

This Quality Manual describes the quality management system implemented
by SPIKA TECH SL to meet the requirements of ISO 13485. The scope of
this quality management system includes the design, development,
marketing, and after-sales service of VR-CARDIO. This system applies to
all activities carried out by SPIKA and encompasses all relevant
processes and functions within the organization.

The Quality Manual provides an overview of the key processes of the
quality management system, as well as the corresponding responsibilities
and authorities within the organization. It also establishes the
documented procedures and resources necessary to ensure compliance with
customer and applicable regulatory requirements.

This manual is a controlled document and is kept up-to-date by Quality
Assurance to reflect changes in the quality management system and to
ensure its continued effectiveness in improving quality and customer
satisfaction.

This quality manual is intended to lay the foundations for SPIKA's
quality management system based on ISO 13485. The following sections of
this standard do not apply:

-   Point 6.4 Work environment and contamination control: As a company
    not directly involved in the manufacturing of medical devices, but
    rather in the development of software, SPIKA does not have
    production facilities where control of the work environment is
    required, such as the implementation of clean rooms that control
    contamination in accordance with the specific standards of
    ISO 13485. Therefore, this point is not relevant to SPIKA's
    activities and processes.

-   Point 7.5.2 Product cleaning: SPIKA focuses on the development of
    medical software that does not require product cleaning processes in
    the context of ISO 13485 requirements. As a result, the point on
    product cleaning is not applicable to SPIKA operations and is not
    included in the Quality Manual.

-   Point 7.5.5 Specific requirements for sterile medical devices: SPIKA
    is not engaged in the manufacture of sterile medical devices nor is
    it involved in processes requiring sterilization. Therefore, this
    point is not relevant to SPIKA's business activities and is not
    addressed in the Quality Manual.

-   Point 7.5.7 Particular requirements for the validation of
    sterilization processes and sterile barrier systems: Since SPIKA is
    not involved in the manufacture of sterile medical devices or
    sterilization processes, this point does not apply to the company's
    operations and is therefore not included in the Quality Manual.

-   Section 7.5.9.2 Specific requirements for implantable medical
    devices: SPIKA is not engaged in the manufacture of implantable
    medical devices, nor is it involved in their development or
    production. Therefore, this section is not relevant to the company's
    activities and is not addressed in the Quality Manual.



-   Point 8.3.4 Rework **:** SPIKA does not carry out manufacturing
    activities or production processes that involve rework of medical
    devices. Since its operations focus on the development of medical
    software, this point is not applicable.

# Definitions and acronyms

-   **Preventive action** : Action taken to prevent the cause of a
    nonconformity from occurring.

-   **Corrective action** : Action taken to eliminate the cause of a
    nonconformity that has occurred.

-   **Audit** : a systematic, independent, documented process for
    obtaining objective evidence and evaluating it objectively to
    determine the extent to which audit criteria are met.

-   **Client** : organization, entity or person that receives a product
    or service.

-   **Approach** **Process-based** : systematic identification and
    management of the processes used in organizations. In particular,
    the interactions between such processes are known as the
    "process-based approach."

-   **Continuous improvement** : recurring activity to improve
    performance.

-   **Procedure:** Description of the operations to be performed, the
    precautions to be taken and the measures to be applied related
    directly or indirectly to the manufacture of a medical product.

-   **Process** : A set of mutually related activities that use inputs
    to provide an intended result.

-   **Product** : The result of a process or set of processes. It can
    also be understood as a service.

-   **Records** : are all those documents derived from the application
    of the quality management system.

-   **Customer satisfaction** : Customer perception of the extent to
    which customer expectations have been met.

-   **System** : set of interrelated or interacting elements.

-   **Traceability** : the ability to track the history, application, or
    location of an object.

-   **CAPA** : from English, corrective action preventive action , in
    Spanish, corrective action preventive action.

-   **DPT** : Job Description.

-   **QMS** : Quality Management System

-   **PNT** : Standard Operating Procedure

# Quality management system

SPIKA has stipulated that the way in which it develops, implements, and
maintains its Quality Management System is in accordance with ISO 13485.

The organization documents a quality management system and maintains its
effectiveness in accordance with the requirements of the aforementioned
standard and those regulations applicable to manufactured products. The
roles performed by the organization are also documented in the same
manner.

That is why the organization follows the requirements shown below:

-   Determines the processes required for the quality management system
    and the application of these processes throughout the organization,
    taking into account the roles played within it.

-   Applies a risk-based approach to process control.

-   Determines the sequence and interaction of the processes.

-   Determines the criteria and methods necessary to ensure both the
    operation and control of the processes so that they are effective.

-   Ensures the availability of resources and information necessary to
    support the operation and monitoring of processes.

-   Implements the necessary actions to achieve planned results and
    maintain the effectiveness of implemented processes.

-   Track, measure, and analyze processes when appropriate and
    necessary.

-   Establishes and maintains the records necessary to demonstrate
    conformity with the international standard and compliance with
    applicable regulatory requirements.

-   Changes in processes are evaluated to determine their impact on
    quality and are controlled to ensure compliance with this standard
    and applicable regulations.

Similarly, when the organization decides to outsource any process that

determine the degree of compliance with the Quality Management System
within the organization, verifying that it has been implemented
according to the planned and documented provisions and is maintained and
effective.

The procedure outlines how the annual internal audit plan is developed,
as well as the internal auditor profile. The audit program is planned
taking into account the status and importance of the processes and
areas, always taking into account the results of previous audits. The
criteria, scope, interval, and audit methods are defined and recorded.
Reports are prepared on the audits performed, and when deemed
appropriate, actions are taken to correct any nonconformities found. The
selection of auditors must guarantee objectivity and impartiality in the
audited area; therefore, auditors cannot audit their own work.

Each audited area is responsible for ensuring that the necessary
corrections and corrective actions are carried out in a timely manner to
eliminate any non-conformities detected.

# Monitoring and measuring processes

SPIKA measures its processes according to the KPIs outlined in procedure
**ESP-006\_process\_measurement,** which clarifies how to measure and
the actions to be taken with SPIKA's key processes. Furthermore, the
annual IRSGCD provides a summary of the achievements obtained from
SPIKA's processes.

# Product tracking and measurement

SPIKA, through the designed work procedures, ensures that appropriate
methods are applied for product monitoring and measurement. Furthermore,
the responsible technician reviews the product's conformity for its
release and marketing. Product release and service provision do not take
place until all planned arrangements have been satisfactorily completed;
every exceptional case outside of this must be properly documented and
analyzed.

# Control of non-conforming product

# Generalities

SPIKA ensures that non-compliant products are identified and controlled
to prevent unintended use or discharge. To this end, the **PNT-016
Approval, Rejection, and Release of Products procedure has been
developed** to provide the instructions, controls, responsibilities, and
authorities related to this process for the identification,
documentation, segregation, evaluation, and disposal of non-conforming
products.

When a non-conforming product is detected, the root cause is identified
through an investigation, and all third parties identified as
responsible for the non-conformity must be notified. This process is
managed using **PNT-004 NON-CONFORMITY MANAGEMENT.** The investigation
aims to:

-   Take action to eliminate the cause and prevent its recurrence

-   Authorize its use, release or acceptance, always duly justified

Records of non-conformities are maintained, such as non-conformity
reports and the actions taken following investigation and determination
of the causes.

# Actions in response to non-conforming product detected before delivery

As mentioned above, the organization addresses nonconforming product by
taking action to eliminate the nonconformity and prevent its recurrence,
to prevent its intended use of a rejected product space, and finally, to
authorize its use, release, or acceptance under concession. Product will
only be accepted under concession if there is a justifiable reason and
applicable regulatory requirements are met.

# Actions in response to non-conforming product detected after delivery

When a non-compliant product is detected after delivery, it will be
withdrawn following procedure **PNT-008 Withdrawal of products from the
market**

# Re- work

At SPIKA, rework operations have not been considered due to the type of
products manufactured.

# Data analysis

SPIKA determines, collects, and analyzes appropriate data to demonstrate
the suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of the quality management
system (QMS). To this end, **PNT-029: Data Analysis has been
established** . This structured document describes the process of
identifying data sources, collecting them, and analyzing them, including
the methods employed, the use of statistical techniques when applicable,
and the scope of the analysis.

# Improvement

# Generalities

SPIKA must continually improve the quality management system through the
quality policy presented here in the Quality Manual, with quality
objectives, audit results, post-market monitoring , data analysis,
corrective and preventive actions, and management review.

# Corrective action

SPIKA takes appropriate action to eliminate the cause of nonconformities
and prevent them from recurring. A procedure, **PNT-006, "Management of
Corrective and Preventive Actions** ," has been developed to define the
requirements for:

-   Review of non-conformities (including complaints).

-   Root cause analysis.

-   Evaluation of actions to prevent recurrence.

-   Planning and documentation of actions and their implementation.

-   Verification that the actions have no adverse effect on the ability
    to meet applicable regulatory requirements or on the safety and
    performance of the medical device.

-   Review of the effectiveness of the actions taken.

# Preventive action

SPIKA takes appropriate action to prevent the causes of nonconformities.
A procedure, **PNT-006, "Management of Corrective and Preventive
Actions** ," has been developed to define the requirements for:

-   Determine potential nonconformities and their causes.

-   Evaluate the need to take action to prevent the non-recurrence of
    non-conformities.

-   Plan and document actions and their implementation.

-   Verification that the actions have no adverse effect on the ability
    to meet applicable regulatory requirements or on the safety and
    performance of the medical device.

-   Review of the effectiveness of the actions taken.
