## **2. Acronyms and definitions**

**Risk:** combination of the probability of occurrence of harm and the severity of that harm.

**Risk management:** systematic application of management policies, procedures and
practices to the tasks of analysing, evaluating, controlling and monitoring risk.

**Injury:** physical lesion, health damages, property or environmental damages.

**Harm:** Health damage, including the damage that can occur from loss of product quality
or availability.

**Hazard:** The potential source of harm.

**Hazardous Situation:** circumstance in which people, property or the environment are
exposed to one or more hazards (ISO 14971)

**Adverse event:** product defect (i.e. malfunction or failure, deterioration in
characteristics or performance, or inadequacy of labelling or of instructions for use) that, directly or indirectly, has led or might have led to serious consequences, namely
deterioration in the state of health of the patient, user or another person. Also called an incident.

**Severity:** A measure of the possible consequences of a hazard.

**Probability of Occurance:** Rank the probability of a failure occurring during the
conception, manufacturing and commercialization of the product

**Risk Assessment:** Set of operations that allows the evaluation of the potential harm
(damage) or injury.

**Risk Analysis:** Study that aims to determine the possible risks that may occur during the
operational phase of a product.

**Risk Control:** Application of a set of operations that allows evaluation, control and
monitoring of risks associated with a particular product or process in order to ensure that the identified risks do not occur or has a very low probability.

**Residual Risk:** Risk remaining after risk control measures have been taken.

**Risk Management Team (RTM):** Representatives of several functions that contribute to
risk evaluation according to their specific knowledge.

**RMT:** Risk Management Team

**RI:** Risk Index

**S:** Severity

**P:** Probability of Occurrence

**RMP:** Risk Management Plan

**RMR:** Risk Management Report

## **5. Review of Risk Management activities**

<div style={{textAlign: "justify"}}>

Risk identification begins by identifying the functional aspects of the device. From this, information about possible problems is collected and a list of potential harms is drawn up.
However, this list cannot be considered exhaustive, as there are risks that are not obvious and can only be detected through subsequent use experience with a wide range of users.

</div>


<div style={{textAlign: "justify"}}>

It is crucial to regularly review and update the risk assessment. This review must be carried out at least once a year and recorded in the risk matrix. Additionally, it should be
updated when necessary, such as in the event of changes to the medical device or when post-sales surveillance data indicates the need for re-evaluation.

</div>


<div style={{textAlign: "justify"}}>

The risk analysis should be based on the experience of operators and feedback from customers and users. Information for risk assessment can be obtained from various
sources, such as customer complaints, satisfaction surveys, customer and user opinions on product performance, identified improvement opportunities, results of post-sales
surveillance and clinical follow-up, technical updates and regulations, expert opinions, usability tests, published standards and incident reports available.

</div>


## **6. Risk Management**

<div style={{textAlign: "justify"}}>

The risk management process is based in ISO 14971 - Medical devices — Application of risk management to medical devices and in Medical Device Regulation (EU) 2017/745. The risk
analysis is performed using the technique FMEA - Failure Mode and Effect Analysis, according to the mentioned in annex B of ISO/TR 24971 - Medical devices — Guidance on
the application of ISO 14971 (as described in PNT-002 Risk Management Procedure).

</div>

## **6.2. Risk acceptance**

<div style={{textAlign: "justify"}}>

Once the evaluation of each risk has been made, the RPN (Risk Priority Number) is obtained by applying the following formula:

</div>


**RPN = P x S**

<div style={{textAlign: "justify"}}>

Each risk estimated must be systematically evaluated, considering that risks should be reduced as far as possible (AFAP) without adversely affecting the benefit-risk ratio.
Therefore, even if the risk is acceptable is necessary to implement measures to bring the risk to its lowest level.

</div>


<div style={{textAlign: "justify"}}>

The acceptability of the resulting RPN is based on pre-established cut-off values, such as:

</div>


Note:


|RPN &lt;4|Acceptable risk|
|---|---|
|RPN≥ 4 but &lt; 15|Tolerable risk|
|RPN≥ 15|Unacceptable risk|



<div style={{textAlign: "justify"}}>

Acceptable Risks - Additional controls can be considered, but are not required.

</div>


<div style={{textAlign: "justify"}}>

Tolerable Risks – Risks should be reduced as far as possible (AFAP).

</div>


<div style={{textAlign: "justify"}}>

Unacceptable Risks - Design changes are needed to mitigate risk, or a benefit-risk analysis is needed to further assess benefit-risk.

</div>


## **6.3. Review of the potential hazard plan**

<div style={{textAlign: "justify"}}>

The Risk Analysis is reviewed at least annually, taking into account the post-production experience gained. The reasons leading to exceptional revisions of the Risk Analysis are
at least the following:

</div>


 - Non-effective control measures: occurrence of danger;

 - Change in the process of product realization: improvements resulting from the previous point, technological changes;

 - Product change;

 - Regulatory and state-of-the-art change;

![](/img/risk-management-plan/Risk-Management-Plan.pdf-7-0.png)
 - New products.

 - After this step, a new version of the Risk Management Report should be issued.

## **6.4. Verification activities**

<div style={{textAlign: "justify"}}>

Verification activities will be carried out to verify whether the risk control has been effectively implemented, and to ensure that no other risks have been introduced and that
the actions implemented actually produce a reduction in risk, and whether that reduction is at its lowest level. The verification activity will be based on the collection of clinical
data and/or studies carried out on other products.

</div>


## **6.5. Production and post-production information**

<div style={{textAlign: "justify"}}>

Information related to the production phase will be collected during the development process and in process control, where risks related to software development can be
identified.

</div>


<div style={{textAlign: "justify"}}>

The collection of information related to the device in the post-production phase aims to obtain information related to the use, safety of the product and other relevant aspects.
This information can be obtained from different sources such as users, complaints, incident reports, etc. The information collected is analyzed and processed and may result
in a change to the list of harms initially identified.

</div>


## **6.6. Usability**

<div style={{textAlign: "justify"}}>

The usability of VR-CARDIO family will be made according to IEC 62366 – Medical devices:
Application of usability engineering to medical devices series.

</div>


<div style={{textAlign: "justify"}}>

This process starts with the product conception and finishes in the end of the product use, passing through the different phases of development, labelling, distribution, and use.

</div>


<div style={{textAlign: "justify"}}>

It’s focused on the product security and try to understand the professional and the factors that can have some influence in the risk increase.

</div>


In this scope, the following parameters must be analyzed:

 - Frequent function of the product.

 - Identification of harms related to the intended use and clinical purpose.

 - Identification of harms related to the intended users

 - Identification of harms related to usability.

 - Identification of characteristics related to the security of the device during the use.

 - Identification of known harms or predictable.

## **6.7. Overall Residual Risk**

<div style={{textAlign: "justify"}}>

If a residual risk does not meet established acceptability criteria, the available data are examined to determine whether the benefits of the intended use outweigh the residual
risk. However, it is also essential to ensure that risks are assessed based on whether they have been reduced to its lowest possible level without negatively affecting the benefitrisk ratio.

</div>


<div style={{textAlign: "justify"}}>

Assessing residual risk may involve collecting and reviewing data on similar medical devices available on the market. If the residual risk is considered acceptable, users should
be informed of significant residual risks.

</div>
