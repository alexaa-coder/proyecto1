## Scope

The Risk Management Report contains the outputs and summary of risk
management activities. The general planning and methods are described in
the Risk Management Plan, while the actual risks are listed and analysed
in the Risk Management Matrix.








**Product information**

**Description**



**Device family name**
VR-CARDIO


**Intended use**
VR-CARDIO family is intended for use by electrophysiologists and
cardiology specialists outside the operating room as an aid to real-time
visualization and interpretation of three-dimensional electro anatomical
maps of cardiac electrophysiology. It provides an immersive,
three-dimensional stereoscopic view of the electrical behavior of the
heart, enhancing non-invasive mapping capabilities during the evaluation
of conditions such as atrial fibrillation and other arrhythmias.   
The system enables the analysis, visualization and informative
interpretation of cardiac electrophysiological data, aiding in the
understanding of complex arrhythmias and guiding preliminary
assessments. It is beneficial in assisting and treating patients at risk
of or being evaluated for cardiac arrhythmias. 
Its use is intended to assist traditional diagnostic methods and to
deepen the understanding of arrhythmia mechanisms in order to develop
non-invasive management strategies. 
The VR-CARDIO family consists of two main components: 


VR-CARDIO Explore (Ref. VR-CARDIO-01) is a web page that receives the
raw data, stores it, launches the algorithms to process the data and
uploads it to the database so that the VR-CARDIO VHET360 can access
it.  




VR-CARDIO VHET360 (Ref. VR-CARDIO-02) is a software system designed
for the real-time visualization and interpretation of three-dimensional
electroanatomical maps of cardiac electrophysiology. It assists
cardiology specialists in diagnosing and treating arrhythmias such as
atrial fibrillation.   




**Market history**
There is no market history. The VR-CARDIO is not yet sold in any
territory.

VR-CARDIO-02

## Risk acceptability

The risk is estimated for each potential harm, resulting in the
combination of the following factors: Severity (S) and Probability of
Occurrence (P) multiplying the different weights for those factors to
obtain the RPN (Risk Priority Number).

RPN = P x S

Each risk estimated must be systematically evaluated, considering that
risks should be reduced as far as possible without adversely affecting
the benefit-risk ratio.

Therefore, even if the risk is acceptable, it is necessary to implement
measures to bring the risk to its lowest level. Note that is not only
enough to bring the risk into the green area, but the risk management
team should also reduce the risk continuously.

In cases, the risk is in a land above the acceptable, but for which is
possible to coexist with, related to the benefits associated and the
impracticability of reducing it, it can be considered a tolerable risk.

The risk assessment will be used for both initial risk assessment and
residual risk assessment.

## Analysis, assessment and risk management

Risk management is an essential stage in the life cycle of a medical
device. This analysis highlights the potential hazardous phenomena
associated with the device, as well as identifying the underlying
causes.

This assessment determines whether, or not, the risks identified are
considered acceptable. Risk acceptability criteria may vary depending on
the specific medical device and the applicable regulations.

The next phase consists of implementing risk control measures.
Appropriate actions are taken to reduce the identified risks to their
lowest possible level. These measures may include design improvements,
the addition of protective devices or the provision of clear warnings to
users.

Once these measures have been implemented, the residual risk is
assessed. This is the level of risk that remains once the risk reduction
measures have been applied. This assessment is followed by a
benefit/risk analysis, which examines whether the potential benefits of
the medical device outweigh the residual risks identified.

Finally, an overall assessment of the acceptability of all residual
risks is carried out. This assessment considers the individual residual
risks as well as their overall contribution to the safety and
performance of the medical device. It determines whether the level of
residual risk is considered acceptable in the specific clinical context.

## Benefit/risk assessment








Benefits

VR-CARDIO family:


Provides detailed and accurate visualization of the heart's
electrical activity, supporting accurate diagnosis and more effective
treatment planning.
Offers an immersive, three-dimensional stereoscopic view and 360°
holographic visualization of the heart's electrical activity.
Enhances non-invasive mapping capabilities for conditions like
Atrial Fibrillation and other arrhythmias, reducing the need for
invasive procedures and associated risks like radiation
exposure.
Aids in identifying arrhythmia mechanisms and supporting
diagnosis and non-invasive management strategies.
Complements traditional diagnostic methods and overcomes
limitations of two-dimensional systems, enabling deeper analysis and
understanding of heart structure and function.
Enables near real-time monitoring of the heart's electrical
activity, improving the efficiency of cardiac care procedures.



Risks

The primary risks identified are related to data quality and user
interpretation, such as inaccurate results from incorrect patient
registration or data acquisition, data loss due to internet connection
issues, or performance loss from inadequate hardware.
There is a risk of over-reliance on the algorithm's predictions,
which is mitigated by the explicit requirement for medical professional
validation of all diagnostic information and decisions.
Cybersecurity risks related to data privacy and unauthorized
access are addressed through login methods, session tokens, and data
encryption.
The device's classification as Class IIa implies that decisions
based on its output should not cause death or an irreversible/serious
deterioration of a person's state of health, nor require surgical
intervention. The software poses no immediate danger to
patients.





## Overall residual risk

All situations with any danger and potential damage identified were
evaluated and the risks were reduced to its lowest possible level. The
overall residual risk associated with the medical device meets the
global residual risk acceptability criteria as there are no individual
residual risks.

All risks were considered acceptable and additional control measures are
not practicable.

After comparing the medical benefits obtained from the use of the device
with the risks, the evidence collected allows us to conclude that the
expected benefits clearly outweigh the risks and, therefore, the device
constitutes added value and should be made available on the market.

Use of the product is free from unacceptable risks when using the device
in accordance with the manufacturer's instructions for use. The medical
device does not raise undue safety concerns; however, the safety and
performance of the device will be continually monitored through ongoing
surveillance activities.

## Conclusion

Based on the established risk management plan, having carried out an
in-depth analysis of the risks associated with VR-CARDIO family and
implementing appropriate control actions, we can conclude that the risks
are now under control. The risk management measures implemented have
effectively reduced the potential risks associated with the use of the
VR-CARDIO family.

It is essential to highlight that patient safety and compliance with
quality standards were taken into consideration in the management of
these risks.

However, it is important to maintain ongoing monitoring and carry out
regular assessments to ensure that risk control measures remain
effective over time. This proactive approach will help ensure device
safety and quality during clinical practice.
