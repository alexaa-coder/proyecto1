# Target

The purpose of this procedure is to define the management of corrective
and preventive actions at Spika Tech, hereinafter SPIKA, which will
result as part of non-conformities, complaints or opportunities for
improvement, as well as any records or feedback from the system.

# Outreach

The scope of this procedure applies to all SPIKA staff with regard to
the management of actions. This procedure establishes the guidelines for
the management of actions and their monitoring and implementation.

#  Responsibilities and Departments concerned

All SPIKA personnel are responsible for carrying out the activities
assigned to them to rectify or prevent non-conformities.

It is the responsibility of the Responsible Technician or his delegate
to monitor the implementation of corrective and preventive measures.

# Definitions and Acronyms

-   Root cause: is the final cause that if corrected would
    prevent recurrence of the event.

-   Corrective action: action to eliminate the root cause of a
    detected non-conformity or other undesirable situation.

-   Preventive action: action to eliminate the possible root
    cause of a potential non-conformity.

-   Correction: action taken to eliminate the non-conforming
    condition / deviation of the affected product or system.

-   SOP: Standard Operating Procedure

-   CAPA: *Corrective Action Preventive Action*.

# Procedure

# Introduction

CAPA is understood as those activities or actions to be developed after
a non-conformity has occurred or carried out to prevent a
non-conformity, corrective and preventive respectively. These actions
will have a responsible and assigned department and a deadline, which
will have been previously established by studying the record that has
led to it, so that all staff in their commitment to quality is
responsible and committed to do it on time. If there are any
impediments, extensions to the initially agreed dates may be arranged.

# Allocation of CAPAs

When non-conformities are opened in SPIKA they can have different
origins, such as:

-   Non-conformities

-   Complaints

-   Internal audits

-   External audits

-   Inspections or audits carried out at SPIKA

-   Outcome or conclusions of quality management system review reports

-   Result any report produced

These activities shall be carried out to avoid recurrences or prevent
possible non-conformities. Therefore, when SPIKA personnel prepare a
document capable of determining a root cause of a non-conformity, they
must request the assignment of a CAPA number to the Technical
Department.

CAPAs may be composed of one or more actions to be performed, so that
there are always concrete actions for each of the root causes found in a
non-conformity.

# Codification of CAPAs

There shall be a database under the custody of the Technical Dept. with
the following content:

-   CAPA No.

-   Origin of the action, here the associated records or documents
    should be indicated.

-   Description of the action

-   Responsible for implementation

-   Enforcement Department

-   Deadline for implementation

-   Extension of the action, if any, first and second extensions of time

-   New deadline, first and second date

The format of the database is attached in **Annex 1** of this procedure.

Each action shall be coded as follows:

-   Corrective action: **AC-XXX/YYY**

-   Preventive action: **AP-XXX/YYY**

-   Extension**: PR-XXX/YYY**

Where XXX shall be a 3-digit sequential number and YY the last two
digits of the current year.

# Registration of a CAPA

Once the necessary investigations or reports have been carried out,
actions can be taken in order to prevent non-conformities or other
events from happening again, which is why the opening of the preventive
or corrective action will be managed, according to **Annex 2 CAPAs**
Template**. This form will be duly signed by the person who is opening**
the action and delivered to the Technical Department, which will review
the content, assign the code and include the action in the database in
order to follow it up.

# Follow-up of actions

On a six-monthly basis, the Technical Manager or the person he/she
delegates will review the list included in the database and the
necessary reminders will be made in writing to the departments that have
actions assigned with a date close to their fulfilment.

Copies of the CAPA forms may also be made available to the responsible
personnel so that they are aware of the contents and details of the
forms, if necessary.

# Date extension

Individuals with assigned CAPAs are responsible for completing them by
the deadline, however, on certain occasions due to impediments,
extensions may be requested with the appropriate extension form which
can be found under **Annex 3 CAPA Date Extension.**
