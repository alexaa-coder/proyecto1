# Target

The objective of this procedure is to establish guidelines on the
systematic approach to ensure compliance of all products with the
approved specifications.

The aim of this procedure is to prevent products that do not meet the
above-mentioned conditions from being used incorrectly in the production
process or from being placed on the market.

# Outreach

This procedure applies to all medical devices that are released at Spika
Tech, hereinafter Spika, regardless of the state of the production
process.

# Definitions and Acronyms

-   Intermediate Product: Partially processed material that still
    has to pass through other stages of production before becoming a
    final product.

-   Finished product: it is understood to be that which has gone
    through all the production stages, including its packaging in the
    final container.

-   Specifications: These describe in detail the requirements to
    be met by the products or materials used or obtained during
    manufacture. They serve as the basis for quality assessment.

# Procedure

# Intermediate product

The intermediate products will be checked against their specifications.
In the same way, they will be placed in the route
https://github.com/SpikaTechSL/VRCardio-VHET360-Visualization (route
only accessible to members of the company as it is a private repository)
until their release by the Responsible Technician.

If the product corresponds in each and every one of its parameters, it
will be declared **APPROVED**. If, on the other hand, there is any
discrepancy between the specifications and the results obtained, a
non-conformity report will be opened to determine whether the product
meets the specifications or not:

-   The discrepancy is a minor defect that does not affect the quality
    of the product itself, the product may be approved.

-   The discrepancy is a major defect that may affect quality, will be
    ruled as **REJECTED/OBSOLETE, will** be identified as such and will
    be located in the path
    https://github.com/SpikaTechSL/VRCardio-VHET360-Visualization (path
    only accessible to members of the company as it is a private
    repository).

In the time after the data acquisition devices are received by the
manufacturer and all information is reviewed by Spika personnel, the
product remains in quarantine and is identified with an orange label.

The intermediate software product shall be approved according to the
model certificate in **Annex 1**. The VR Cardio product consists of
software, which is released by the relevant Spika staff and approved by
the Technical Manager, following the certificate in **Annex 1.**

# Finished products

Once the product has been finalised, the compilation must be checked to
see if it has been done according to the product specifications, the
variable data corresponds and the intermediate product is duly approved,
in which case the product will be approved and released for sale.

If discrepancies exist, a Non-Conformity report should be initiated for:

-   To rule if the discrepancy is minor without affecting the quality of
    the product and its final result, under the authorisation of the
    Responsible Technician, is that the product is **APT** for release
    and supply to the market.

-   Ruling if the discrepancy is major and adversely affects safety or
    the product must be reprocessed before sale, it may be **REJECTED**
    or **APPROVED** after reprocessing. In both cases, under the
    supervision and approval of the T. Responsible.

In order for the product to be approved and released to the market, it
is necessary to:

-   Intermediate versions are approved as indicated in **Annex 1**.

-   The final version produced complies with the software
    reproducibility procedure (**Annex 2)** that ensures that the
    released software can be delivered at the point of use without
    deformation or unauthorised changes and with correct installation.

-   The software can be downloaded and installed according to **Annex
    3.

-   The labelling of the software complies with regulatory requirements
    **(Annex 4).**

-   The technical manager has reviewed all of the above procedures to
    ensure that the software is working properly and can be released to
    the market **(Annex 5).**
