# Aim

The purpose of this procedure is to define and establish Spika Tech's
(hereinafter Spika) post-market surveillance system for the detection,
evaluation, analysis, and reporting of incidents, adverse events, and/or
adverse reactions related to its medical devices. This system ensures
patient safety, complies with applicable regulatory requirements, and
implements corrective measures when necessary.

# Scope

This procedure applies to medical devices distributed by SPIKA.

#  Responsibilities and Departments Affected

The Technical Manager will be responsible for supervising the
surveillance system, where his or her duties, together with the
company's General Management, will be:

-   They will notify the Competent National Authority of Field Safety
    incidents and Corrective Actions provided that the notification
    criteria are met.

-   Investigate incidents and take necessary corrective actions

-   They must keep the Notified Body informed of any issues that may
    affect certification and arise during the post-production phase.
    This will include any relevant changes generated by the surveillance
    system.

-   Coordination of actions with the client upon notification of an
    incident related to the products

-   Acting as an interlocutor with the client and/or health authorities
    when required

-   Collaborate with the client or health authorities in providing
    relevant information about the products distributed by SPIKA

# Definitions and Acronyms

-   Product deficiency : Any inadequacy of the identity, quality,
    durability, reliability, safety or effectiveness of a product,
    including malfunction, errors in use or inadequacy of information
    provided by the manufacturer.

-   Recall: Any measure intended to prevent the further marketing
    of a product that is in the supply chain.

-   Field Safety Corrective Action (FSCA): A Field Safety
    Corrective Action is an action taken by a manufacturer to reduce the
    risk of death or serious deterioration in health associated with the
    use of a medical device that has already been placed on the market.
    Such actions will be notified in the form of a Field Safety Notice.

-   Field Safety Notice (FSN): A communication to customers
    and/or users issued by the Manufacturer or its representative
    regarding a Field Safety Corrective Action.

-   Adverse event (AE): An event that is harmful to the health of
    a subject receiving pharmacological treatment without indicating a
    causal relationship with the treatment. Any malfunction, failure, or
    deterioration in the characteristics or functioning of a product, as
    well as any deficiency in the labeling or instructions for use that
    may or may have resulted in death or serious deterioration in the
    health of a patient or user.

-   Adverse Reaction (ADR): Any harmful and unexpected reaction
    to the administered medications (regardless of the dose
    administered).

-   Damage : physical injury or damage to the health of people or
    damage to property or the environment.

-   Error in use : An act or omission of an act that has had a
    result different from that intended by the manufacturer or expected
    by the operator of the medical device.

-   Improper use : Action or omission by the operator or user of
    a medical device as a result of conduct outside of what is intended
    by the Manufacturer in its risk control.

-   Reasonably foreseeable misuse : Use of a product or system in
    a manner not intended by the manufacturer, but which may result from
    easily predictable human behavior.

# Procedure

For each product or family of Medical Devices designed and manufactured
by SPIKA , a call must be made available in the event of a possible
adverse event or reaction. In these cases, the Surveillance System
Manager, in collaboration with the other departments as appropriate,
must:

-   Gather information and provide the information requested in relation
    to the manufacturing of the affected batches

-   Will collaborate in the investigation associated with the incident

-   If this is determined after the investigation, the company will
    manage the reconciliation of stored products and contact those
    customers who have the product for the appropriate management in
    each case.

    This procedure ensures post-production surveillance and proper
    communication with regulatory authorities when incidents or adverse
    effects related to medical devices manufactured by SPIKA are
    detected.

# Generalities

The Medical Device Surveillance System aims to facilitate the direct,
early, and harmonized implementation of field safety corrective actions
across all Member States where a specific device is used, rather than
country-by-country measures.

Corrective actions include, but are not limited to: product recall;
issuance of a Field Safety Notice; additional monitoring/modification of
products in use; modification of a product's future design, components,
or manufacturing process; modification of labeling or instructions for
use.

All records generated through this procedure (initial reports, final
reports, justifications for non-notification, follow-up reports, and
trend analyses) will be managed in accordance with the current document
and record control procedure, retained for at least 10 years, and made
available to the competent authorities upon request.

# Role of the manufacturer and incident reporting system

The Manufacturer or its Authorized Representative must submit an initial
incident report to the Competent National Authority for registration and
evaluation. Each initial report will result in a final report, unless
the initial and final reports are merged into one.

However, not all incident reports will lead to corrective action. As a
general principle, if there is any doubt about whether an incident
should be reported, there should be a willingness to report rather than
not. These considerations will be noted in the report, and if the
manufacturer chooses not to report, they will be reflected in the
manufacturer's documentation.

In the case of Spain, the AEMPS will be notified using the MFIR form
included in Annex 1 of PNT014 Post-marketing monitoring or depending on
the severity according to the Annexes included in the MEDDEV 2.12 Guide
in its current version and/or on the AEMPS portal at the following
address:


Incidents occurring outside the EEA and Switzerland that do not require
a Field Safety Corrective Action in these geographical areas will not be
reported. Incidents occurring outside the EEA and Switzerland that would
result in a Field Safety Corrective Action in these geographical areas
must be reported as a Field Safety Corrective Action.

# Criteria for reporting incidents

Events that meet criteria A, B and C described below are considered
reportable incidents:

1.  The Event has occurred, that is, where the following have occurred:

-   A malfunction or deterioration in the characteristics or
    deterioration of the product

-   False positive or false negative results

-   Unforeseen adverse reactions or side effects

-   Interaction with other products/substances

-   Product degradation/destruction (e.g., catching fire)

-   Inadequate therapy

-   Inaccuracy in labeling, instructions or advertising maintenance
    (e.g. omissions)

1.  The product is suspected of having contributed to the incident: To
    determine the link between the product and the incident, the
    manufacturer must take into account:

-   Professional opinion

-   Preliminary evaluation results

-   Evidence of similar incidents that occurred in the past

-   Any other evidence

1.  The event has occurred or could have resulted in one of the
    following situations:

-   Death of a patient, user or other person

-   Serious deterioration in the health status of a patient, user or
    other person

# Conditions for the notification of periodic summaries to the Surveillance System

The frequency and format of the periodic reports will be agreed upon
with the competent authority.

However, the following activities will be taken into account in these
cases:

-   Incidents described in a Field Safety Note (FSN) : Individual
    notifications of incidents described in FSNs are not required
    provided Spika has issued the relevant corrective actions. The
    frequency and content of these notifications will also be agreed
    upon with the relevant authorities.

-   Common and well-documented incidents : those included in the
    product risk analysis, these may be exempted from reporting and may
    be reported in the periodic summaries.

# Circumstances that do not require notification to the system.

These are those discovered by the user before using the product, where
the event is caused by the patient's condition, because the useful life
or expiration date of the medical device has been exceeded, when the
protection against a failure is functioning correctly, or when
foreseeable or expected side effects appear.

# Trend Reports

Spika will be alert to potential increases or changes in the trend of
product incidents and when a notable increase in incidents or a trend is
observed, of:

-   Reportable incidents

-   Incidents exempt from mandatory notification

-   Events that are not normally reportable

Notification must be made to the competent authorities, regardless of
what has been agreed upon with the modality of periodic summaries that
has been agreed upon.

To make trend reports, a format is included in **Annex 2** , which is
recommended for use in preparing trend reports.

# Notifications of usage errors and misuse

Spika will be alerted to all reported complaints and will evaluate all
potential cases of misuse and improper use. This evaluation must be
based on risk management, the design of operating conditions, design
validation, and corrective and preventive action procedures.

It will be noted that there are usage errors that must be reported, such
as those that have resulted in death or serious deterioration of health,
or that pose a serious threat to public health.

When they do not result in death or serious impairment and are due to
misuse, there is no need to report it.

# Calendar for reporting an incident 

Serious incident associated with marketed products:

-   It must be reported immediately after a causal relationship between
    the incident and the product has been established or is reasonably
    possible. No later than (maximum) 15 calendar days after becoming
    aware of the incident.

Serious threat to public health:

-   It must be reported immediately, and in no case later than 2 days
    (calendar days) after becoming aware of the incident.

Death or serious and unforeseen deterioration of health:

-   It must be reported immediately after identifying the relationship
    between the incident and the product (or if the relationship is
    suspected), and in no case later than 10 days (calendar days) after
    becoming aware of the incident.

Other incidents:

-   They must be notified immediately after identifying the relationship
    between the incident and the product, and in no case later than 15
    days (calendar days) after having become aware of the incident.

# Who should be notified 

Serious incident notifications must be sent to the National Competent
Authority of the country where the incident occurred. In Spain, this
involves notifying the Spanish Agency for Medicines and Medical Devices
(AEMPS) using the appropriate form, as detailed in Regulation (EU)
2017/745.

# Managing user reports that arrive at Spika

Spika may receive reports from users. In these cases, they will be
treated as if they had been detected by Spika. This procedure will be
applied in full, and the initial incident report will be sent provided
that the relevant reporting criteria are met.

If Spika considers that the criteria are not met, the justification will
be sent to the Competent National Authority.

# Research

In general, Spika will conduct the investigation into incidents, using
whatever tools it deems appropriate, and the Competent Authority will
monitor progress.

If Spika is unable to conduct an investigation into the incident, it
must immediately notify the Competent National Authority. In this case,
the Authority may intervene or initiate an independent investigation if
it deems appropriate. In these cases, Spika will provide the relevant
information to the authorities upon request.

Spika may require, and it is recommended, access to the product, i.e.,
the unit that caused the incident, both for the investigation and to
determine whether the incident is genuine. Every effort will be made to
obtain access to the product.

Any change in the integrity of the units must be reported to the
authorities, especially if it would affect subsequent product analysis.

# Results of investigations and follow-up

Any change in the integrity of the products involved must be reported to
the competent authorities without delay, especially if it affects the
subsequent analysis of the product. Once the investigation is complete,
Spika will initiate all necessary measures, including consultations with
the Competent National Authority and the implementation of any Field
Safety Corrective Actions (FSCA).

Spika will prepare a final report containing the results of the
investigation and the actions taken, ensuring compliance with the
requirements set out in Article 87 of Regulation (EU) 2017/745. If the
investigation exceeds the established deadline, a follow-up report will
be generated.

The final report will consist of a written statement of the results of
the investigation and the measures taken, which may include:

-   Absence of measures

-   Additional monitoring of products in use

-   Preventive measures in future production

-   Field Safety Corrective Action (FSCA)

The report format is included in **Annex 1.**
