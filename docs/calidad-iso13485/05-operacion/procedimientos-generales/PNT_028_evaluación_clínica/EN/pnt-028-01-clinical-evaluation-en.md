# Aim

The purpose of this procedure is to provide a system for performing the
clinical evaluation of medical devices produced at Spika Tech,
hereinafter SPIKA.

# Beat

The procedure applies to medical devices produced by SPIKA management.

#  Responsibilities and Departments involved

-   It is the responsibility of the TD/PRRC and M to select the Clinical
    Evaluator.

-   It is the responsibility of the Clinical Evaluator to perform the
    clinical evaluation and prepare the Clinical Evaluation Report.

-   It is the responsibility of the TD/PRRC and M to approve and sign
    the Clinical Evaluation Report.

-   It is the responsibility of M and the Clinical Evaluator to sign the
    Declaration of Interests.

# Definitions and acronyms

-   CEP : Clinical Evaluation Plan

-   CER : Clinical Evaluation Report

-   M : Management

-   MD : Medical Device

-   TD : Technical Director

-   PRRC : Person Responsible for Regulatory Compliance

-   M : Management

# Procedure

# Clinical evaluator

The M will designate one or more individuals to conduct the clinical
assessment, known as the Clinical Evaluator. The Clinical Evaluator need
not be a SPIKA employee **.**

The Clinical Evaluator must have a higher education degree in the
respective field and five years of documented professional experience;
or ten years of documented professional experience if a degree is not a
prerequisite for the specific role.

The clinical evaluator, with respect to the particular device under
evaluation, should have knowledge of the device technology and its
application, the diagnosis and management of the conditions the device
is intended to diagnose or manage, and knowledge of medical
alternatives, treatment standards, and technology (e.g., specialized
clinical experience in the relevant medical specialty).

As a general principle, clinical evaluators should possess knowledge of
at least the following:

-   Research methodology (including clinical research design and
    biostatistics).

-   Information management (e.g. scientific training or library
    qualification).

-   Experience with relevant databases, such as Embase and Medline.

-   Requirements regulatory .

-   Medical writing (e.g., postgraduate experience in a relevant science
    or medicine; training and experience in medical writing, systematic
    review, and clinical data evaluation).

The clinical evaluator should be familiar with the Medical Device
Regulation (MDR) guidelines, MEDDEV 2.7.1./rev 4 and other relevant
regulatory requirements.

The Clinical Evaluator must submit the CV to the SPIKA Ministry.

The Clinical Evaluator must complete a Declaration of Interest. This
Declaration should include relevant financial interests related to the
medical device for which the clinical evaluation is being conducted, in
addition to their current work as an evaluator. The Declaration of
Interest must be dated and signed by both the evaluator and the SPIKA M.

# Clinical evaluation

The clinical evaluator performs the clinical evaluation in order to
obtain and evaluate all available clinical data on the medical device or
equivalent devices to determine whether the device or system is safe and
effective, and to demonstrate the device's compliance with the general
safety and performance requirements (Annex I of MDR 2017/745).

The clinical evaluator conducts the clinical evaluation based on a
thorough analysis of available pre- and post-marketing clinical data
relevant to the intended use of the device in question, including
clinical performance and safety data. During the clinical evaluation,
the clinical evaluator must include in the analysis both the specific
medical device in question and any data relating to products declared
equivalent by the manufacturer.

The clinical assessor must perform the clinical assessment during the
medical device conformity assessment process, prior to its marketing and
market launch. To perform the clinical assessment, the Clinical Assessor
must prepare the Clinical Assessment Plan (CEP). Once the clinical
assessment is completed, the Clinical Assessor must prepare the Clinical
Assessment Report (CER). Once the medical device is placed on the
market, the clinical assessor must perform the clinical assessment
periodically, as new clinical information regarding the safety and
performance of the device during use becomes available. The information
resulting from the clinical assessment is included in the ongoing risk
analysis and may lead to changes in the use of the device.

The clinical evaluator must perform the clinical evaluation at least
once a year for implantable medical devices and Class III medical
devices, and every two years for other medical devices.

# Clinical evaluation plan

The Clinical Evaluator is responsible for preparing the Clinical
Evaluation Plan.

The Clinical Evaluation Plan must include information on the safety or
performance of the medical device that is generated from the use of the
device, which is obtained from the following:

-   Clinical investigation(s) of the device in question.

-   Clinical investigation(s) or other studies reported in the
    scientific literature, of a device for which equivalence with the
    device in question can be demonstrated.

-   Reports published in peer-reviewed scientific literature on other
    clinical experience with the device in question or a device for
    which equivalence to the device in question can be demonstrated.

-   Clinically relevant information from post-marketing surveillance ,
    in particular from post-marketing clinical follow-up .

# Clinical evaluation report

The Clinical Evaluator is responsible for conducting literature research
and preparing the Clinical Evaluation Report and Literature Research
Protocol.

The Clinical Evaluation Report must detail:

-   The technology on which the medical device is based, the intended
    use of the device, and any claims made about the clinical
    performance or safety of the device.

-   The nature and extent of the clinical data that have been evaluated.

-   How the referenced information (recognized standards and/or clinical
    data) demonstrates the clinical performance and safety of the device
    in question.

-   That the device(s) operate as intended by the manufacturer.

-   That the device(s) do not pose any undue security risk.

-   That the risks associated with the use of the device(s) are
    acceptable when compared to the benefits to the patient.

The Clinical Evaluation Report will be signed and dated by the
evaluator(s) and accompanied by the manufacturer's justification for the
choice of evaluator.
