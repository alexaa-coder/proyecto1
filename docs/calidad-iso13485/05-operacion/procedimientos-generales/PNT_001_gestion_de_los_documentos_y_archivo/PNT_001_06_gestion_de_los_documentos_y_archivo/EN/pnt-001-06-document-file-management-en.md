# Aim

The purpose of this procedure is to establish a system that ensures that
the documentation that is in force and to which authorized users have
access is the most up-to-date and that all modifications made to the
processes and documentation are promptly notified to the appropriate
personnel in each case, thus ensuring a correct flow of information
between personnel and constant maintenance of their training.

Guidelines for preparing procedures for the initial drafting and/or
updates of all SpikaTech documents are available in **Annex 1 of this**
procedure.

# Definitions and Acronyms

-   **PNT** : Standard Operating Procedure.

-   **GF** : Manufacturing Guide

-   **TR** : Technical Manager

-   **DO** : Director of Operations

-   **DG** : Director General

-   **E** . Specification

-   **PV** : Validation Protocol

-   **PC** : Qualification Protocol

-   **Controlled Copy:** is the copy generated from copy number 1 that
    is considered the original and is kept in the file as the original.

-   **Working Copy** : is the copy generated from copy number 1 of a
    document for data recording, see for example manufacturing or
    packaging guides.

-   **Unauthorized or uncontrolled copy:** a copy of any document whose
    distribution is not subject to control.

-   **Obsolete version:** any document that is replaced by a new version
    of the same document.

-   **Current document:** A document that has been approved after
    passing through a controlled documentation review-approval process
    and on which the relevant personnel have been trained. It is
    therefore the most up-to-date version and is accessible to those
    users who have already been trained on it.

-   **Review:** Verification that what is written corresponds to what is
    established. A document's reviewer will be a person with sufficient
    knowledge of the applicable topics. A document may have one or more
    reviewers from one or more departments.

-   **Approval:** Consideration of the prepared document as valid or
    acceptable. Approval also involves reviewing it in whole or in part.
    Approvers will be department heads (or staff to whom they delegate)
    whose work may be affected by the content or whose knowledge of the
    information contained in the document is required.

# Procedure

# General document management

All standard operating procedures, protocols, specifications, etc., will
not be effective until they have been previously reviewed and approved
by the appropriate individuals. Spika Tech's quality management system
will be digitally stored in the company's shared space.

The distribution of controlled copies will be made by recording them in
the electronic controlled copy registration box, following the format in
**Annex 3** .

If distribution to third parties is required, it will be sent by email
with acknowledgment of receipt in all cases. These must be kept as
evidence of delivery to the third party. If no response is received from
the third party within 15 days, the document will be considered approved
by them.

# Elaboration

The guide indicated in **Annex 1** and the PNT Template indicated in
**Annex 2 will be used** .

When necessary, any Spika Tech employee may request the Responsible
Technician or the person they delegate to provide the template for the
document they need to prepare in an editable format, as well as the
document code. Documents may include annexes, the format of which is
already included in the procedure template.

All documents will start at revision 01, with no versions 00 existing
under any circumstances.

The author of the document will be responsible for requesting the
coordination of reviewers and approvers to carry out their task. Once
signed by all, the document will be delivered to the Responsible
Technician or the person they delegate for document management.

# Distribution

Once the document is approved, after being duly reviewed and approved
according to the table shown in section *5.1.2* ., the following actions
will be triggered:

1.  Delivery of the document to the Responsible Technician or the person
    he delegates

2.  The responsible Technician or the person he delegates must:

    1.  Record the document in the copy logbook

    2.  Upload copy 1 of the document to the shared space for staff
        reading.

3.  The departments involved in the document have 15 days to read and/or
    review the document and sign and date the reading log, which will
    accompany the procedure, and/or complete the corresponding
    questionnaire.

4.  After 15 days, the Responsible Technician or the person designated
    by him/her must verify that all applicable personnel have read or
    been trained in the procedure. In the following cases:

    1.  Negative: People who have not read/formulated the document will
        be contacted and will be given a one-week extension.

    2.  Affirmative: the document will be considered valid for its entry
        into force.

# Reading recognition

In the case of Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs), the corresponding
departments must read and understand them. A digital form (Google Forms)
will be used.

This form will record that the person has read the procedure,
automatically recording their full name, code, version, and title of the
procedure read.

# Obsolete documents

When a document becomes obsolete because a new version has been
generated, the Responsible Technician or the person delegated by him/her
must:

-   Take the controlled copy logbook of that version of the document and
    proceed to remove all of them.

-   Delete the copy from the shared space and move it to the obsolete
    folder.

# Backup documentation

By archiving documentation, the Technical Manager or the person
designated by him or her keeps all procedures up to date. Likewise, all
current documents are stored on SPIKA's information storage server:
[Spika Tech - Home](https://spikatech.sharepoint.com/sites/SpikaTech/)

# Review date

All SpikaTech procedures will be valid for three years from the date
they come into effect.

This 3-year period may be shortened if a circumstance or change occurs
beforehand that makes it necessary to review and modify it.

It is recommended that changes made to a document for the purpose of
issuing a new version be indicated in italics. This is intended to
facilitate quick viewing of the changes made compared to the previous
version. If the entire document is modified, the use of italics may be
avoided, indicating this in the reasons for the modification.

If the three-year period is reached and the procedure does not require
review because the conditions explained in the document have not
changed, an ex officio review may be carried out. To do this, fill out
the corresponding space in the ex officio review form, following the
format in **Annex 4.** This will explain the reason for the extension.
Up to two ex officio reviews may be issued, each valid for three years.
The new effective date must be stamped on all pages of the original, as
well as on the cover of the document in the "valid until" box. In these
cases, it will not be necessary to distribute controlled copies.

# Model management

The templates will always be included as part of the procedures, serving
as a guide for preparing the documents. The responsible technician or
the person they delegate will have editable templates for the documents
and will make them available to interested parties at all times.

# Database

The Technical Manager or the person designated by him or her will
maintain a database with the codes, titles, effective dates, and
revisions of all documents. To formalize this database, a signed and
dated copy will be issued for each modification.

# Document translations

When Spika Tech medical devices are marketed in countries whose official
language is not Spanish, documents intended for the user or healthcare
professional (instructions for use, labels, manuals, technical sheets,
etc.) must be translated into the corresponding language of the
destination country.

The Responsible Technician or the person he delegates will be
responsible for:

-   Ensure the technical accuracy of translations, including review by
    competent personnel or qualified translators.

-   Keep the translated versions as part of the technical file.

-   Identify translated documents using the same code as the original
    document plus the ISO 639-1 language code (e.g., IFU-012.01\_EN,
    IFU-012.01\_FR).

-   Ensure that they are distributed together with the product in the
    corresponding country.

Translations will be subject to the same documentary control as other
controlled documents. In the event of discrepancies, the Spanish version
will prevail as a technical reference.

# Archive

It is important to archive the documentation generated from activities
related to Medical Devices correctly and securely, ensuring rapid access
and ensuring that the documentation is complete, controlled, and
restricted.

# File content

The documentary archive will contain the following:

-   Documentation relating to the quality system and specifications of
    each product.

-   Documentation that allows for tracking of products within the
    production and control chain.

-   Documentation that allows understanding of the product's design and
    features.

-   Documentation related to marketing, containing the product's
    identification data (trade name, version), production date, delivery
    to distributors or health centers

-   Documentation relating to experience gained from the use of the
    products, including that derived from the surveillance system, as
    well as complaints and market returns.

-   Complete technical documentation, including the technical file,
    clinical evaluation, and risk management in accordance with
    harmonized standards, aligned with Annex II of Regulation (EU)
    2017/745.

-   Risk management procedures in accordance with the applicable
    harmonized standard.

-   Procedures that guarantee product traceability, including a unique
    product identification system.

-   Technical documentation on post-marketing surveillance, aligned with
    Annex III of Regulation (EU) 2017/745.

# Custody of documentation

Documentation derived from the quality management system is considered
confidential, and access to it is restricted to authorized individuals,
who are responsible for its handling and review. It may not be
photocopied, reproduced, or transferred to any other person without
explicit reason.

The following persons are authorized to access said documentation:

-   Technical Manager

-   Director of Calida

-   SPIKA Address

No person other than those mentioned above will have access to this
documentation, with the following exceptions:

-   Pharmaceutical Inspectors from the Ministry of Health, Social
    Services and Equality, with prior accreditation and accompanied by
    one or more authorized persons.

-   Internal auditors of the Company, duly accredited and accompanied by
    one of the persons authorized to have access.

-   External auditors of the Company, with prior authorization from the
    Technical Director and accompanied by one of the persons authorized
    to have access.

-   Inspectors from an official body, specifically authorized by the
    Spanish Administration for this purpose, with an explicit reason and
    accompanied by one of the persons authorized to access them.
