# Aim

The objective of this procedure is to define the way in which audits are
carried out at Spika Tech, hereinafter SPIKA, which are necessary to
verify the degree of compliance with the quality standards and
regulations applicable to the departments that make up SPIKA as well as
the suppliers that provide a good (product or service) to SPIKA, since
the quality of SPIKA's products depends not only on the organization
itself but also on all the materials and services that make up the
manufactured product.

*Three types of audits* are distinguished throughout the procedure :

-   **GRADE 1: Internal** , also known as **self-inspections** , where
    SPIKA departments are audited internally in order to periodically
    check the efficiency and effectiveness of the Quality Management
    System.

-   **GRADE 2: To suppliers:** to various suppliers, regardless of the
    material to be supplied, as well as to various services, always
    impacting the quality of the products supplied. Evaluation
    questionnaires are also available as annexes to this procedure,
    through which an initial assessment is conducted to assess
    compliance with the desired quality standard.

-   **GRADE 3:** **External** : those received by organizations, health
    authorities or official or private entities, to SPIKA

# Scope

This procedure applies to all suppliers listed as having a potential
impact on product quality and to departments involved in the production
and maintenance chain of the Quality Management System.

#  Responsibilities and Departments Affected

It is the responsibility of all SPIKA staff to collaborate in the
execution of internal audits.

It is the responsibility of the Responsible Technician to coordinate the
correct execution of the internal and external audit plan, as well as
the performance of the audits themselves.

# Definitions and Acronyms

-   **Non-Conformity (NC):** An audit finding that does not meet a
    requirement specified in an audit criterion.

-   **Finding:** The result of evaluating the audit evidence collected
    against audit criteria. Audit findings may indicate either
    conformity or nonconformity with audit criteria, as well as
    opportunities for improvement.

-   **Recommendation:** A recommendation for improving quality, safety,
    or effectiveness. It does not constitute a deviation.

-   **CAPAs:** Corrective Action Preventive Action

# Procedure

# Introduction

*three types of audits* are distinguished : internal, supplier and
external.

The planning phases are:

-   **Preparation and planning** : An agenda should be sent to the
    auditee and a date agreed upon.

-   **Audit execution** : The audit will be carried out by management or
    the person delegated by the management, except for the audit of the
    technical department itself, which will be audited by a different
    department within the company or an external entity. Once the audit
    is completed, the observations detected should be discussed with the
    audited personnel.

-   **Report preparation** : After execution, a corresponding report
    should be prepared. It is recommended to follow a clear format and
    follow the production flow of the processes to be reviewed. Compile
    the reviewed items and the observations with their classification.
    Any information deemed necessary for a proper understanding of the
    report may also be attached.

-   **CAPAs Plan** : If observations are found that require resolution,
    a CAPAS plan will be generated and coding will be assigned according
    to **PNT-006 Management of Corrective and Preventive Actions** .

# Grade 1: Internal Audits/Self-Inspections

The points to be reviewed in each area will depend on the type of
activities carried out there. However, some aspects are common and
should be taken into consideration in all areas subject to inspection,
as applicable:

-   The staff.

-   Premises/ Facilities/ Equipment.

-   Documentation: Standard Operating Procedures and Methods,
    Specifications, Manufacturing Guides, etc.

-   Organization of the department or area.

-   Validations of procedures.

-   Processes.

It should be noted that self-inspections do not consist of an exhaustive
and detailed review of each of the parts and activities that make up a
specific department or area, but rather are based on the evaluation of a
**representative sample** of the inspected process or area, and the
deviations detected refer to what was observed.

# Frequency

Self-inspections will be carried out at least **once a year** , although
if necessary due to the severity or number of observations found, the
frequency of implementation could be increased.

# Audit staff 

Self-inspections will be conducted by internal personnel unless the
Technical Manager decides to subcontract them to a third party external
to the organization. Auditing personnel must be trained in the company's
procedures and have completed the qualifications included in **Appendix**
2.** This record must be authorized by the Technical Manager before the**
audit is conducted. It will be kept in the Quality Assurance files. The
selection of auditors must guarantee objectivity and impartiality in the
audited area; therefore, auditors cannot audit their own work.

# Grade 2: To suppliers

The supplier's impact must be previously assessed, the impact on product
quality must be taken into account, therefore at least the following
suppliers will be applicable, however, a recommendation for the
evaluations is indicated, but a **risk assessment document must be**
issued** to define whether the audit is necessary or not, if such a**
document does not exist, the recommendation indicated below will be
followed:

-   Critical materials that make up the products manufactured at SPIKA.

-   Other materials/services

It is recommended that the initial assessment questionnaires included in
the annexes of this procedure be available in order to understand the
auditee's quality system, although the supplier's own documentation may
be available.

In cases where an on-site audit of the vendor is not possible (e.g.,
cloud service providers such as Google, Amazon, or Microsoft, SaaS
software providers, or global mission-critical services providers), a
justification for not performing an audit based on the risk analysis
will be documented.

-   Alternatively, the supplier may be evaluated by:

-   Recognized certifications (e.g. ISO 27001, ISO 13485, SOC 2, etc.),

-   Publicly available third-party audit reports,

-   Specific contractual conditions (quality agreements, security or
    confidentiality clauses),

-   Supplier document review, if applicable.

This evaluation must also be approved by the Responsible Technician and
recorded as part of the supplier's evaluation file.

# *Grade 3: External*

*External audits will be those in which an official or private body or
entity audits SPIKA. Therefore, to the extent possible, given that they
depend on a third party, they will be scheduled and it will be desirable
to receive an agenda from that third party. However, it will be at the
discretion of the auditors/inspectors to give advance notice and notify
the corresponding visit.

*Spika Tech will always be willing to accept audits when necessary, as a
general rule, as a symbol of its commitment to the implemented quality
system.

*The nature and scope of the audit, as well as the classification of
nonconformities, will be made available to the auditors/inspectors. Once
the reports are received, SPIKA undertakes to assess the nonconformity
and nonconfirmation in order to implement the appropriate corrective
actions. This will be managed using procedure **PNT-006, Management of**
Corrective and Preventive Actions.***

non-conformity found as shown below:

# Audit report

For the final issuance of the Audit Report, the auditing staff will have
a maximum of three months from the date of the audit. If any of the
nonconformities detected are Critical (C), these deadlines may be
reduced in order to immediately correct the finding.

The reports will be coded as follows:









INTERNAL AUDIT
EXTERNAL AUDIT
Where:




**AUD -INT-XX/YY**
**AUD-EXT-XX/YY**
XX corresponds to the audit's consecutive number in the current
year


YY corresponds to the current year of the audit




The report numbers will be provided in the corresponding database under
the custody of the responsible technician or the person delegated by
him/her.
