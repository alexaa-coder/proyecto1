## Manufacturer’s Declaration

➢ We declare that







`☐` no application has been lodged with any other notified body for the same device-related quality management

system.
`☐` we have provided HTCert with all information about any previous application for the same device-related quality
management system.

➢ Specifically for legacy devices that can benefit from the extended transitional period, we declare that

`☐` the certificates under Directive 93/42/EEC (MDD) covering the listed products were issued after 25 May

2017, were valid on 26 May 2021 and have not been withdrawn afterwards.
`☐` for devices that did not require the involvement of a notified body under the MDD the declaration of conformity
was drawn up prior to 26 May 2021.
Additionally, we declare that

`☐` the certificates were still valid on 20 March 2023
`☐` the certificates expired before 20 March 2023 and a competent authority has granted a derogation from the
applicable conformity assessment procedure according to MDR Article 59 (1)
`☐` the devices continue to comply with MDD
`☐` there are no significant changes in the design and intended purpose
`☐` the devices do not present an unacceptable risk to the health or safety of patients, users or other persons, or to

other aspects of the protection of public health
`☐` a QMS according to MDR Article 10 (9) is implemented

➢ We undertake to fulfil the obligations imposed by the quality system approved.

➢ We undertake to keep the approved quality system adequate and effective.

➢ We undertake to keep up to date the post-market surveillance system and the PMCF plan.

➢ We undertake to fully comply with the vigilance requirements of Regulation (EU) 2017/745 (MDR) and to report, to the

competent authorities and HTCert of the following incidents:

    - any serious incident involving devices made available on the Union market, except expected side-effects clearly
documented in the product information which are subject to trend reporting.

    - any field safety corrective action in respect of devices made available on the Union market, including any field

The reports will be submitted

    - immediately after we establish the causal relationship between the incident and the device or that such causal
relationship is reasonably possible and not later than 15 days after we become aware of the incident.

    - in the event of a serious public health threat: immediately and not later than 2 days after we become aware of that
threat.

    - in the event of death or an unanticipated serious deterioration in a person's state of health: immediately after we
establish or as soon as we suspect a causal relationship between the device and the serious incident but not later
than 10 days after the date on which we become aware of the serious incident.

➢ We undertake to perform all the necessary investigations in relation to any serious incident and the devices concerned

and to fully co-operate with the competent authorities and where relevant with HTCert during the investigations.

➢ We undertake the obligation to notify the competent authorities and HTCert on any statistically significant increase in

the frequency or severity of incidents that are not serious incidents or that are expected undesirable side- effects.

➢ We undertake to comply with all other requirements following from the Regulation (EU) 2017/745 (MDR).

➢ We authorize HTCert to carry out all the necessary audits and supply it with all relevant information.

➢ We grant access to HTCert staff and representatives to all business premises without prior notice and we have agreed

on this right of access with all critical suppliers and subcontractors involved in development, production, testing and
storage for their business premises.

➢ We agree to notify HTCert of any planned changes that could affect the certified management system or the certified

products.

➢ We agree to comply with the General Terms and Conditions of HTCert.

➢ We agree to pay all applicable fees as per the certification offer, the General Terms and Conditions and HTCert price

list. We understand that non-payment of fees will result in withdrawal of approval and we undertake to pay the fees
charged even when the tasks performed result in an undesirable outcome from our point of view.

➢ We understand and accept that HTCert has the right to suspend, restrict or withdraw certificates issued and the duty to

fulfil its information obligations according to the MDR requirements. In addition, in the case of legacy devices for which
the surveillance has been transferred to HTCert, we understand and accept that HTCert has the right to suspend,
restrict or withdraw certificates issued by the MDD Notified Body.

➢ We declare that all data mentioned in this application, the appendices, the Quotation Questionnaire and all

accompanying documents provided are truthful and correct.
