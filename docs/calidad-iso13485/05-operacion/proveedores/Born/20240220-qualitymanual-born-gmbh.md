We produce and distribute textiles of all kinds, including technical, medical and lifestyle textiles, as
well as orthoses.

**Products from Born GmbH**

Born GmbH, knitwear for fashion & engineering, based in Dingelstädt, is entered in the commercial
register as a limited liability company. The company is registered at the local court 07745 Jena under
the commercial register number HRB 401195. The company is economically active. The last change in
the commercial register was made on 06.04.2022. The company is managed by a managing director.
The proportion of women in management is approx. 65 per cent. The company is also active in
foreign trade/export. The export share of turnover is included in our company data. The company
operates from its location in Dingelstädt (DE). Processed items from the Polish partner company in
Wielun (PL) are treated as items from an outsourced process and subjected to appropriate quality
assurance measures.
### **2. Corporate policy & objectives**


We have established a _**quality policy**_ that fulfils the high expectations and wishes of our customers.
The success of our customers and employees is at the centre of our actions. This policy forms the
basis for our dealings with customers, employees, partners, resources and our environment. The
quality management system is continuously improved.


The voluntary commitment to continuous improvement (CIP) applies to all processes. Appropriate
documentation and review of the effectiveness of all resulting measures is carried out regularly.


**Customers**
Our aim is to provide products and services that fully satisfy our customers, both in terms of the
products themselves and through short delivery times and high delivery reliability. Added to this is
our high degree of flexibility in individual and series production, as well as the diversity of our fields
of activity, which gives us stability even in difficult economic times. Comprehensive, customerorientated advice is also a matter of course for us.


**Employees**
We are very proud of our high-performance team of highly motivated and well-trained specialists.
They are highly committed to realising individual customer requirements and identify strongly with
our company and our quality objectives.

Our processes are divided into three process levels. Sub-processes are defined within these
processes. Sub-processes are procedures within a process that lead to the expected result.


**Management process**


The management process represents a clearly defined structure of our company. The management
process is used to determine the input and requirements of interested parties and provide the
necessary resources.


**Value creation process**


Our value creation process is based on the requirements of our customers. The required inputs and
expected results as well as criteria and procedures (including monitoring, measurements and the
associated performance indicators) are defined for this value creation process. This ensures the
effective implementation and management of this process.


**Support process**


The support process is provided to ensure that the value creation process runs smoothly. This
ensures the realisation of customer requirements.


The interaction of the processes was determined and visualised in the process map.


**Litigation risks**


Potential process risks are assessed at least once a year or as required. Internal risk management is
carried out using defined guidelines, e.g. on the basis of the relevant process instructions (SOP).
Appropriate measures to minimise risks are defined based on the respective quality requirements of
the products and processes if risks are identified. Their effectiveness is measured and evaluated as
part of internal audits and management reviews. Customer-specific risk management requirements
are also reflected, see Appendix 2.

### **5. Measurement, analysis and improvements**


The effectiveness of the system is monitored by analysing customer satisfaction and the results of
regular internal audits.


The management system is subject to planned and unplanned audits. These audits use objective
evidence to determine compliance with the applicable instructions and the effectiveness of the
measures taken. Necessary corrective measures are initiated and their implementation monitored.


The QM system is evaluated and further developed with the regular management review.


The fulfilment of customer requirements is ensured by measuring and monitoring the processes.


Corrective and improvement measures are systematically introduced in the ongoing sub-processes
and their implementation is monitored.
